## 概述

Jared Kaplan 在接受时代周刊采访时讨论了 Anthropic 负责任扩展政策的改革。他认为「我们停止训练对任何人都没有帮助」，且「如果竞争对手在飞速前进，我们单边承诺没有意义」。转变是从硬性护栏转向更大透明度——承认使单边暂停困难的竞争动态。

---

## 要点

### 停止训练对任何人都没有帮助

- Anthropic 单边暂停不会阻止行业进步
- 竞争对手会继续——Anthropic 会落后
- 安全研究受益于处于前沿
- 对竞争动态的务实看法

### 单边承诺没有意义

- "如果竞争对手在飞速前进，单边承诺没有意义"
- 协调问题——每个人都需要一起行动
- Anthropic 此前曾承诺 RSP——对可行性的修正看法
- 需要行业范围或政府主导的协调

### 从硬性护栏转向透明度

- RSP 2.0 或改革：减少对硬性暂停的强调
- 更多强调透明度：发布评估、能力、风险
- 实现外部审查和问责
- 承认自我监管的局限

### 竞争现实

- AI 实验室处于竞赛中——资本、人才、算力
- 单边克制代价高昂
- 政策和监管可能是协调所必需的
- 行业无法单独解决

### 持续安全投入

- 改革不意味着放弃安全
- Anthropic 继续投入评估、可解释性、对齐
- 透明度作为一种问责形式
- 不同方法，相同目标

# RSP Overhaul: From Hard Guardrails to Transparency

## Summary

Jared Kaplan discussed Anthropic's overhaul of its Responsible Scaling Policy in an interview with TIME. He argued that "it wouldn't help anyone for us to stop training" and that "it didn't feel it made sense for us to make unilateral commitments if competitors are blazing ahead." The shift was from hard guardrails to greater transparency—acknowledging the competitive dynamics that make unilateral pauses difficult.

---

## Key Points

### Wouldn't Help Anyone to Stop Training

- Unilateral pause by Anthropic would not stop industry progress
- Competitors would continue—Anthropic would fall behind
- Safety research benefits from being at the frontier
- Pragmatic view of competitive dynamics

### Unilateral Commitments Don't Make Sense

- "Didn't feel it made sense to make unilateral commitments if competitors are blazing ahead"
- Coordination problem—everyone needs to move together
- Anthropic had previously committed to RSP—revised view of what's feasible
- Need for industry-wide or government-led coordination

### Shift from Hard Guardrails to Transparency

- RSP 2.0 or overhaul: less emphasis on hard pauses
- More emphasis on transparency: publishing evaluations, capabilities, risks
- Enables external scrutiny and accountability
- Acknowledges limits of self-regulation

### Competitive Reality

- AI labs are in a race—capital, talent, compute
- Unilateral restraint is costly
- Policy and regulation may be necessary for coordination
- Industry cannot solve this alone

### Continued Safety Investment

- Overhaul does not mean abandoning safety
- Anthropic continues to invest in evaluations, interpretability, alignment
- Transparency as a form of accountability
- Different approach, same goals
