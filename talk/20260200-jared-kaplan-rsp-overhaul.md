# 独家：Anthropic 放弃旗舰安全承诺

## 完整内容

### 放弃核心承诺

Anthropic，这家将自己定位为顶级研究实验室中最注重安全的 AI 公司，正在放弃其旗舰安全政策的核心承诺，公司官员告诉时代周刊。2023 年，Anthropic 承诺除非能提前保证公司的安全措施足够，否则绝不训练 AI 系统。多年来，其领导人将这一承诺——负责任扩展政策（RSP）的核心支柱——作为证据，证明他们是一家负责任的公司，能够抵御市场激励，不会急于开发可能危险的技术。但最近几个月，公司决定彻底改革 RSP。这一决定包括废除在 Anthropic 无法提前保证适当风险缓解时不发布 AI 模型的承诺。

### Kaplan 的声明

「我们觉得我们停止训练 AI 模型对任何人都没有实际帮助，」Anthropic 首席科学官 Jared Kaplan 在独家采访中告诉时代周刊。「在 AI 快速发展的背景下，我们真的不觉得如果我们单边承诺……而竞争对手在飞速前进，对我们来说有意义。」

### 新政策内容

时代周刊审查的新版政策包括承诺对 AI 的安全风险更加透明，包括对 Anthropic 自身模型在安全测试中的表现进行额外披露。它承诺匹配或超越竞争对手的安全努力。它承诺，如果领导人既认为 Anthropic 是 AI 竞赛的领导者，又认为灾难风险重大，则「延迟」Anthropic 的 AI 开发。但总体而言，对 RSP 的修改使 Anthropic  far less 受自身安全政策约束，此前这些政策明确禁止在适当安全措施尚未到位时训练超过一定水平的模型。

### 商业成功背景

这一变化发生在 Anthropic  riding 一连串技术和商业成功的高潮之际。此前被认为在 AI 竞赛中落后于 OpenAI 的 Anthropic，其 Claude 模型，尤其是软件编写工具 Claude Code，赢得了大量忠实粉丝。2 月，Anthropic 筹集了 300 亿美元新投资，估值约 3800 亿美元，并报告其年化收入以每年 10 倍的速度增长。该公司直接向企业销售的核心商业模式被许多投资者认为比 OpenAI 主要通过庞大消费者用户群变现的主要策略更可信。

### Kaplan 否认向市场压力屈服

Anthropic 高管兼联合创始人 Kaplan 否认公司改变路线是对随着超级智能竞赛加速的市场激励的屈服。他将其框架为对新兴政治和科学现实的务实回应。「我不认为我们在做任何 U 型转弯，」Kaplan 说。

### 2023 年的希望与 2026 年的现实

当 Anthropic 在 2023 年推出 RSP 时，Kaplan 说，公司希望它能鼓励竞争对手采用类似措施。（没有竞争对手做出如此公开的暂停 AI 开发的承诺，但许多人发布了详细说明其缓解风险计划的长篇报告，Kaplan 将其归功于 Anthropic 对行业的良好影响。）高管还希望这种方法可能最终成为具有约束力的国家法规甚至国际条约的蓝图，Kaplan 声称。但这些法规从未实现。相反，特朗普政府支持对 AI 开发放任自流的态度，甚至试图废除州法规。没有联邦 AI 法律在望。虽然 2023 年全球治理框架似乎可能，但三年后情况已经明朗，那扇门已经关闭。与此同时，AI  supremacy 的竞争——公司之间以及国家之间——只会加剧。

### 评估科学的复杂性

更糟糕的是，AI 评估的科学被证明比 Anthropic 首次制定 RSP 时预期的更复杂。强大新模型的到来意味着，2025 年 Anthropic 宣布无法排除这些模型促进生物恐怖袭击的可能性。但他们无法排除的同时，也缺乏强有力的科学证据表明模型确实构成那种危险，这使得难以说服政府和竞争对手他们看到的谨慎行动的必要性。公司此前想象的可能是清晰红线的东西，反而正在聚焦为模糊的渐变。

### 近一年的讨论

Kaplan 说，近一年来 Anthropic 高管讨论了重塑旗舰安全政策以匹配这一新环境的方法。他们不断回归的一点是 founding premise：要进行适当的 AI 安全研究，他们必须构建处于能力前沿的模型——尽管这样做可能加速他们担心的危险的到来。根据 Kaplan，2 月 Amodei 决定，在竞争对手飞速前进时阻止公司训练新模型对任何人都没有帮助。「如果一个 AI 开发者暂停开发以实施安全措施，而其他人在没有强有力缓解的情况下继续训练和部署 AI 系统，可能导致一个更不安全的世界，」经 Amodei 和 Anthropic 董事会一致批准的新版 RSP 在其引言中写道。「保护最弱的开发者将设定节奏，负责任的开发者将失去进行安全研究的能力。」

### 外部评估

METR 政策主任 Chris Painter 是一家专注于评估 AI 模型风险行为的非营利组织，在 Anthropic 许可下审查了政策的早期草案。他说这一变化可以理解——但也是世界应对潜在 AI 灾难能力的看跌信号。对 RSP 的修改表明 Anthropic「相信需要将其安全计划转入分诊模式，因为评估和缓解风险的方法跟不上能力发展的步伐，」Painter 告诉时代周刊。「这是社会尚未准备好应对 AI 带来的潜在灾难性风险的更多证据。」

### 保留的激励

Anthropic 认为重新设计的 RSP 旨在保留旧政策的最大好处。例如，通过约束自己发布新模型，Anthropic 的原始 RSP 也激励它快速构建安全缓解措施。（因为否则公司将无法向客户销售其 AI。）Anthropic 表示相信可以维持这一激励。新政策承诺公司定期发布所谓的「前沿安全路线图」：列出其希望构建的未来安全措施的详细目标的文件。

### 风险报告

Anthropic 表示还将承诺每三到六个月发布所谓的「风险报告」。公司表示，这些报告将「解释能力、威胁模型（模型可能构成威胁的具体方式）和主动风险缓解如何配合，并提供整体风险水平的评估」。这些文件将比公司已经发布的报告更深入，发言人告诉时代周刊。Painter 说：「我喜欢对透明风险报告和可公开验证的安全路线图的强调。」但他表示「担心」从先前 RSP 下的二元阈值转向——某种能力的到来可能充当暂时停止 Anthropic AI 开发的触发器——可能 enable「温水煮青蛙」效应，危险缓慢上升而没有单一时刻拉响警报。

### Kaplan 的辩护

当被问及 Anthropic 是否向市场压力屈服时，Kaplan 认为，事实上 Anthropic 正在对安全开发 AI 做出 renewed 承诺。「如果我们所有竞争对手在灾难性风险方面都透明地做正确的事，我们承诺做得一样好或更好，」他说。「但我们认为在其他人继续前进而我们实际上没有向生态系统贡献任何额外风险的场景下，停止参与 AI 研究、AI 安全，并最有可能作为理解技术前沿的创新者失去相关性，对我们来说没有意义。」

# Exclusive: Anthropic Drops Flagship Safety Pledge

## Metadata

|-------|-------|

| **Platform** | TIME |
| **Speaker** | Jared Kaplan (Chief Science Officer, Anthropic) |

## Full Content

### Dropping the Core Pledge

Anthropic, the wildly successful AI company that has cast itself as the most safety-conscious of the top research labs, is dropping the central pledge of its flagship safety policy, company officials tell TIME. In 2023, Anthropic committed to never train an AI system unless it could guarantee in advance that the company's safety measures were adequate. For years, its leaders touted that promise—the central pillar of their Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP)—as evidence that they are a responsible company that would withstand market incentives to rush to develop a potentially dangerous technology. But in recent months the company decided to radically overhaul the RSP. That decision included scrapping the promise to not release AI models if Anthropic can't guarantee proper risk mitigations in advance.

### Kaplan's Statement

"We felt that it wouldn't actually help anyone for us to stop training AI models," Anthropic's chief science officer Jared Kaplan told TIME in an exclusive interview. "We didn't really feel, with the rapid advance of AI, that it made sense for us to make unilateral commitments … if competitors are blazing ahead."

### New Policy Contents

The new version of the policy, which TIME reviewed, includes commitments to be more transparent about the safety risks of AI, including making additional disclosures about how Anthropic's own models fare in safety testing. It commits to matching or surpassing the safety efforts of competitors. And it promises to "delay" Anthropic's AI development if leaders both consider Anthropic to be leader of the AI race and think the risks of catastrophe to be significant. But overall, the change to the RSP leaves Anthropic far less constrained by its own safety policies, which previously categorically barred it from training models above a certain level if appropriate safety measures weren't already in place.

### Commercial Success Context

The change comes as Anthropic, previously considered to be behind OpenAI in the AI race, rides the high of a string of technological and commercial successes. Its Claude models, especially the software-writing tool Claude Code, have won legions of devoted fans. In February, Anthropic raised $30 billion in new investments, valuing it at some $380 billion, and reported that its annualized revenue was growing at a rate of 10x per year. The company's core business model of selling direct to businesses is seen by many investors as more credible than OpenAI's main strategy of monetizing a vast consumer user base.

### Kaplan Denies Capitulation to Market Pressure

Kaplan, the Anthropic executive and co-founder, denied the company's decision to change course was a capitulation to market incentives as the race for superintelligence accelerates. He framed it instead as a pragmatic response to emerging political and scientific realities. "I don't think we're making any kind of U-turn," Kaplan says.

### 2023 Hopes vs. 2026 Reality

When Anthropic introduced the RSP in 2023, Kaplan says, the company hoped it would encourage rivals to adopt similar measures. Executives also hoped the approach might eventually serve as a blueprint for binding national regulations or even international treaties. But those regulations never materialized. Instead, the Trump Administration has endorsed a let-it-rip attitude to AI development. No federal AI law is on the horizon. And while a global governance framework may have seemed possible in 2023, three years later it has become clear that door has closed. Meanwhile, competition for AI supremacy—between companies but also between nations—has only intensified.

### Evaluation Science Complexity

The science of AI evaluations has proven more complicated than Anthropic expected when it first crafted the RSP. The arrival of powerful new models meant that, in 2025, Anthropic announced it could not rule out the possibility of these models facilitating a bio-terrorist attack. But while they couldn't rule it out, they also lacked strong scientific evidence that models did pose that kind of danger. What the company had previously imagined might look like a bright red line was instead coming into focus as a fuzzy gradient.

### Year-Long Discussion

For nearly a year, Anthropic executives discussed ways to reshape their flagship safety policy to match this new environment. One point they kept coming back to was their founding premise: the idea that to do proper AI safety research, they had to build models at the frontier of capability. In February, according to Kaplan, Amodei decided that keeping the company from training new models while competitors raced ahead would be helpful to nobody. "If one AI developer paused development to implement safety measures while others moved forward training and deploying AI systems without strong mitigations, that could result in a world that is less safe," the new version of the RSP states.

### External Assessment

Chris Painter, director of policy at METR, reviewed an early draft of the policy with Anthropic's permission. He says the change is understandable—but also a bearish signal for the world's ability to navigate potential AI catastrophes. The change shows Anthropic "believes it needs to shift into triage mode with its safety plans, because methods to assess and mitigate risk are not keeping up with the pace of capabilities."

### Retained Incentives

Anthropic argues the retooled RSP is designed to keep the biggest benefits of the old one. The new policy commits the company to regularly release "Frontier Safety Roadmaps" and "Risk Reports" every three to six months.

### Kaplan's Defense

Asked whether Anthropic was caving to market pressure, Kaplan argued that Anthropic was making a renewed commitment to developing AI safely. "If all of our competitors are transparently doing the right thing when it comes to catastrophic risk, we are committed to doing as well or better," he said.
