# 达里奥·阿莫迪做客 The Times Tech 播客

**日期：** 2024年12月20日

**平台/场合：** The Times Tech 播客（英国泰晤士报）

**演讲者：** 达里奥·阿莫迪，Anthropic 首席执行官

**时长：** 44 分钟

## 背景/语境

达里奥·阿莫迪做客 The Times Tech 播客，伦敦泰晤士报的技术播客。英国受众和时机——2024 年底——将对话置于戏剧性 AI 进展和英美欧政策关注增长的一年语境中。44 分钟的时长允许进行实质性讨论，同时比多小时播客露面更 accessible。泰晤士报的技术报道聚焦商业、政策和社会影响。

## 要点

- **未来愿景：** 阿莫迪将「强大 AI」描述为带来可能让人「感动落泪」的「黄金未来」——对积极转型的 emotionally charged 愿景

- **为何离开 OpenAI：** 讨论了他离开 OpenAI 创立 Anthropic 的经历，包括希望构建一个将安全作为结构性优先而非众多考虑之一的组织

- **AI 加速进展：** 描述了 AI 如何加速多个领域的进展：
  - **生物学：** 药物发现、生物系统理解、解决疾病和衰老的潜力
  - **健康：** 个性化医疗、诊断、治疗优化
  - **神经科学：** 理解大脑、心理健康治疗
  - **经济发展：** 贫困地区的跨越式发展、减少不平等
  - **治理：** 改善决策、减少冲突、增强集体智慧

- **乐观与谨慎的平衡：** 保持了他 characteristic 的平衡——表达对积极潜力的 genuine 兴奋，同时承认风险和谨慎发展的必要性

- **英国和欧洲语境：** 探讨了英国和欧洲语境下的 AI 发展和监管，包括美国实验室与国际政策的关系

## 重要引言

- 「强大 AI 将带来黄金未来——一个可能让人感动落泪的未来。」

- 关于积极潜力：「我们在生物学、健康、神经科学、经济发展、治理方面看到的加速——是真实的。」

- 关于离开 OpenAI：「我想构建一个安全不可协商的地方。」

## 总结

The Times Tech 播客露面提供了阿莫迪观点的更 concise 版本，特别强调积极愿景。他的「黄金未来」和「感动落泪」语言是他最 emotionally expressive 的之一——suggesting 对 transformative  upside 的 genuine 信念，而不仅仅是技术可能性。关于为何离开 OpenAI 的讨论强化了他将 Anthropic 定位为结构上安全优先的叙事。AI 加速进展的领域列举（生物学、健康、神经科学、经济发展、治理）呼应了「慈爱机器」文章。英国 venue 带来了国际维度，探讨这些发展如何影响以及在美国以外如何被 perceive。44 分钟的长度使该集比他的多小时播客露面更 accessible，同时仍涵盖实质性内容。

# Dario Amodei on The Times Tech Podcast

**Date:** December 20, 2024

**Platform/Venue:** The Times Tech Podcast (The Times, UK)

**Speaker:** Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic

**Duration:** 44 minutes

## Background/Context

Dario Amodei appeared on The Times Tech Podcast, the technology podcast of The Times of London. The UK audience and the timing—late 2024—placed the conversation in the context of a year of dramatic AI progress and growing policy attention in both the US and Europe. The 44-minute format allowed for substantive discussion while remaining more accessible than multi-hour podcast appearances. The Times' tech coverage focuses on business, policy, and societal impact.

## Key Points

- **Vision of the Future:** Amodei described "powerful AI" as bringing a "golden future" that could move people "to tears"—an emotionally charged vision of positive transformation

- **Why He Left OpenAI:** Discussed his departure from OpenAI to found Anthropic, including the desire to build an organization with safety as a structural priority rather than one consideration among many

- **AI Accelerating Progress:** Described how AI is accelerating progress across multiple domains:
  - **Biology:** Drug discovery, understanding of biological systems, potential to address disease and aging
  - **Health:** Personalized medicine, diagnostics, treatment optimization
  - **Neuroscience:** Understanding the brain, mental health treatments
  - **Economic Development:** Leapfrogging development in poorer regions, reducing inequality
  - **Governance:** Improving decision-making, reducing conflict, enhancing collective intelligence

- **Balance of Optimism and Caution:** Maintained his characteristic balance—expressing genuine excitement about positive potential while acknowledging risks and the need for careful development

- **UK and European Context:** Addressed AI development and regulation in the UK and European context, including the relationship between US labs and international policy

## Notable Quotes

- "Powerful AI will bring a golden future—one that could move people to tears."

- On positive potential: "The acceleration we're seeing across biology, health, neuroscience, economic development, governance—it's real."

- On leaving OpenAI: "I wanted to build something where safety wasn't negotiable."

## Summary

The Times Tech Podcast appearance offered a more concise version of Amodei's views, with particular emphasis on the positive vision. His "golden future" and "move people to tears" language was among his most emotionally expressive—suggesting genuine belief in transformative upside, not just technical possibility. The discussion of why he left OpenAI reinforced his narrative of Anthropic as safety-first by structure. The enumeration of domains where AI accelerates progress (biology, health, neuroscience, economic development, governance) echoed the "Machines of Loving Grace" essay. The UK venue brought an international dimension, addressing how these developments affect and are perceived outside the US. The 44-minute length made the episode more accessible than his multi-hour podcast appearances while still covering substantive ground.
