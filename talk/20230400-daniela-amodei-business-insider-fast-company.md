## 概述

Anthropic 总裁 Daniela Amodei 在两篇专访中阐述：信任与安全不是 AI 开发的约束，而是核心产品功能。她解释「有用、诚实、无害」（HHH）框架、宪法 AI、从 Stripe 到 OpenAI 再到 Anthropic 的职业路径，以及将安全融入研究每一步的理念。Fast Company 文章强调她在 2020 年即关注 AI 安全，并描述她如何平衡研究、商业与公司建设。

---

## 商业内幕文章要点（中文）

- **信任与安全是功能**：「如果信任与安全被视为与所有其他团队平等的合作伙伴，我认为这不一定会造成摩擦。」
- **职业路径**：全球健康与政治 → 国会竞选 → Stripe（2013，约 40 人时加入）→ OpenAI（2018）→ Anthropic（2020，与 Dario 等七人离开）。
- **离开 OpenAI**：创立 Anthropic 围绕「小而整合的团队、以安全为核心的研究赌注」的愿景。
- **HHH 与宪法 AI**：利用不同人群提供反馈、构建「宪法 AI」——用人类原则训练模型，鼓励透明与无害，使 AI 能自我监督。
- **展望**：需同时关注今日问题与未来问题；「我们正处于某种真正重大、可能改变我们沟通与交往方式的边缘」。

---

## Fast Company 文章要点（中文）

- **2020 年的担忧**：在 OpenAI 任安全与政策 VP 时，Amodei 已认为需要对生成式 AI 风险有更全面的关注。
- **Anthropic 差异化**：以安全、可解释、「可引导」为核心；Claude 获好评；已融资约 16 亿美元。
- **HHH 指导**：推动大模型日益「有用、诚实、无害」；各团队分别负责相关性、减少幻觉、限制有害输出。
- **角色**： oversight 多数日常管理，高级领导向她汇报；将 Dario 的技术愿景转化为运营规范。
- **双轨焦点**：Daniela 是 Anthropic 研究+商业双轨的落实者，负责保持两者平衡。

## Business Insider Article (April 24, 2023)

**By Stephanie Palazzolo**

*Editor's note: This story was originally published on April 24, 2023*

In December last year, Twitter CEO Elon Musk disbanded the company's trust and safety council despite rising reports of hate speech on the site.

The controversial choice triggered a fiery debate in Silicon Valley around the purpose of trust and safety teams. For many, such groups stood in opposition to progress, serving to slow down product innovation and introduce cumbersome rules and hurdles, especially in a world that lived by Mark Zuckerberg's infamous "move fast and break things" motto.

However, the majority of Anthropic cofounder and president Daniela Amodei's career has been spent trying to prove the opposite: **that trust and safety is a feature, not a bug.**

"It's an organizational structure question, but it's also a mindset question," she told Insider. "So if trust and safety is viewed as an equal partner to all of these other teams, I don't think that necessarily causes friction."

Of course, Amodei's Anthropic is a completely different animal than Musk's Twitter, but the fields of artificial intelligence and social media can conjure up similar questions around who's allowed to police technology and designate which values are deemed "right" and "wrong."

And AI, like social media, has become the topic du jour in recent months, as accelerating tech advancements and startup formation fuel growing interest from investors, founders, and the general public alike.

Anthropic, a rival AI lab to OpenAI, has benefitted from this hype. Already, the company has raised over $1 billion in funding, with its latest $300 million round from Spark Capital — its second raise in 2023 alone, according to PitchBook — landing it a $4.1 billion valuation, The Information reported.

Now, Amodei, alongside her Anthropic colleagues, is working to ensure that this new era of AI has trust and safety at its core, rather than as an afterthought.

### Off the beaten path

Amodei's journey to tech was more unorthodox than most.

She originally began her career in global health and politics, even helping to win a Congressional campaign in northeast Pennsylvania. However, after a few months on Capitol Hill running scheduling and communications for congressman Matt Cartwright, she realized that politics wasn't a fit.

She pivoted to tech in 2013, joining then-under-the-radar payments startup Stripe when it was just 40 people strong, before moving to OpenAI in 2018. In both companies, her roles centered around people, risk, and safety, which would become overarching threads throughout the rest of her career.

In 2020, Amodei and six other OpenAI employees, including her brother Dario Amodei, left the company to start rival AI lab Anthropic. The decision was mired in controversy: former OpenAI employees told The Wall Street Journal that Dario Amodei, then OpenAI's lead safety researcher, was worried that the company's deal with Microsoft would push it to release products too quickly without the proper safety testing and would tether it too closely to the tech giant.

Amodei told Insider that OpenAI's product was too early in its development during her time there to comment on, but added that the founding of Anthropic centered around a **"vision of a really small, integrated team that had this focused research bet with safety at the center and core of what we're doing."**

And it seems like the company has already made moves towards that vision — in March, it released Claude, a "more steerable" alternative to OpenAI's ChatGPT, which companies like Notion and Quora have already began using.

### Safety as a first priority

Many AI companies today claim they're focused on safety, but Amodei says that Anthropic's dedication goes beyond lip service.

Safety, she told Insider, is a value that needs to be baked into every step of the research process, rather than just a final step.

Anthropic itself uses a **"triple H" framework — helpful, honest, and harmless** — in its research. In practice, this means leveraging a range of different people and perspectives when providing feedback on model outputs, or reinforcement learning, as well as building "constitutional AI," or models trained with a set of human-provided principles that encourage transparency and harmlessness, she said. Guided by these principles, AI will be able to supervise itself and deem whether model outputs satisfy the "triple H" framework without needing as many humans involved, she explained.

Additionally, Anthropic publishes its safety research in hopes that other groups, from academic labs to government actors, will use it as well, she added.

Although Amodei believes that trust and safety is a product requirement for customers nowadays, she also acknowledges that it creates a tough trade-off between speed and safety for companies looking to turn a profit. However, she told Insider that prioritizing these values from day one can help companies remain nimble without getting bogged down by unforeseen crises down the road.

### Looking forward

Though the company's founding centered around the idea of a "small, integrated team," Anthropic already has over 100 employees and has seen an over 50% increase in headcount over the past six months, according to LinkedIn.

Throughout Anthropic's growth, the company has kept an interdisciplinary culture, with employees whose experiences range from physics to computational biology to policy writing, Amodei told Insider. Even its founders hail from nontraditional backgrounds — cofounder Jack Clark was a tech journalist at Bloomberg before pivoting to AI.

"We don't look like a traditional tech company," she said.

For Amodei, the issue of building AI safely requires a near-impossible undertaking: predicting the future.

"We all have to simultaneously be looking at the problems of today and really thinking about how to make tractable progress on them while also having an eye on the future of problems that are coming down the pike," she said.

But even with all the uncertainty, she says that there's a lot to be excited about.

"Working in it doesn't make you immune to this sense that we're on the cusp of something really big and potentially transformative to how we communicate and engage with other people," she said.

## Fast Company Article (September 2023)

**By Mark Sullivan**

**Title: Anthropic's Daniela Amodei is keeping AI from spinning out of control**

In 2020, long before the AI arms race fully seized hold of Silicon Valley, Daniela Amodei was already worried about AI safety.

Working as a VP of safety and policy at OpenAI—the company that would eventually launch ChatGPT—Amodei saw the need for a more comprehensive focus on mitigating the risks inherent in generative AI. She felt the inner workings of AI models should be more explainable, and their outputs reliably ethical and bias-free. That led Amodei and her brother Dario—then the VP of research at OpenAI—to strike out with their own AI research company, Anthropic, in 2021.

The Amodeis had their own ideas about how AI should be built, safeguarded, and distributed to the world (hint: not via Microsoft). But Anthropic's main differentiator from OpenAI would be a central focus on making AI models safe, explainable, and "steerable" (that is, adaptable based on human feedback). As president of Anthropic, Daniela directs the work of a number of trust and safety research groups at the company. The most conspicuous result of Anthropic's approach is a ChatGPT-like chatbot called Claude, which has received glowing reviews for its writing and summarization abilities. Claude doesn't yet have the name recognition of ChatGPT, but deep-pocketed investors seem keen to arm Anthropic with the cash to make a serious run at OpenAI. The company has raised $1.6 billion to date, says Crunchbase.

Anthropic's guiding light, she says, is pushing its large language models toward being ever more **"helpful, honest, and harmless."** She works to instill those values into the various research teams she oversees. Some teams work on making sure Claude provides information that's relevant to the user's prompt, others focus on limiting AI chatbots' penchant for "hallucinating," or making things up, while others focus on limiting harmful or toxic outputs. "No model available today is 100% safe, but our goal for Claude is helping to improve on each of those three criteria," she says.

Her trust and safety work goes back to her days at the payments company Stripe, and extends through OpenAI, then Anthropic. "I've done everything from managing people functions to managing technical teams—engineering and research teams," she says. At Anthropic, however, her duties extend well beyond that. **"I oversee the majority of the day-to-day management of the company, so the senior leadership team reports to me."**

Amodei is very close to her brother (and Anthropic CEO) Dario, who, in fact, played a key role in developing the landmark GPT-3 foundation model at OpenAI. "I personally think Dario is the most impressive technical visionary in this space," she says. "I really view my job as helping to take that vision that Dario and other technical leadership have, and help to actually translate it into sets of operating norms—how the researchers work together, how we build things into a product and how we translate that into a business."

With her work on both the business and safety research sides of the business, Daniela is the actuation of Anthropic's dual focus, and the person most accountable for keeping the two sides in balance.
