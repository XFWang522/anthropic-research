# AI 可解释性的紧迫性

**日期：** 2025年4月

**平台/场合：** 发表于 darioamodei.com 和 LessWrong 的文章

**演讲者：** 达里奥·阿莫迪，Anthropic 首席执行官

**时长：** 长篇文章

## 背景/语境

「AI 可解释性的紧迫性」是达里奥·阿莫迪论证 AI 可解释性研究已变得 critically urgent 的文章。发表于其个人网站和 LessWrong，文章在 earlier 关于机制可解释性（理解神经网络内部如何工作）的讨论基础上，以更大紧迫感 framing。时间——2025 年 4 月——反映了他的观点：在 AI 变得 overwhelmingly powerful 之前「 steering 巴士」的窗口正在关闭。文章将可解释性与多个风险类别联系起来，论证 recent 突破使研究 feasible，但 AI 进展快于可解释性进展。

## 要点

- **关键紧迫性：** AI 可解释性不仅重要——它 critically urgent。在 AI 系统变得 overwhelmingly powerful 之前发展理解的窗口有限

- **「驾驭巴士」窗口：** 在 AI 变得如此 powerful 以至于我们失去控制之前，我们仍能「驾驭巴士」。可解释性对驾驭 essential——你需要理解一个系统才能控制它

- **近期突破：** 可解释性研究取得了 significant 进展。近期突破 demonstrate 了理解神经网络内部是 feasible 的，而非不可能

- **竞赛：** AI 进展快于可解释性。能力快速增长，而我们的理解滞后。这创造了危险的差距

- **现代 AI  fundamentally 不透明：** 与传统软件不同，现代 AI 是「生长」而非「构建」的。我们不显式编程行为；我们训练 develop emergent 能力的系统。这使得可解释性 essential 而非可选

- **目标：「AI 的 MRI」：**  aspirational 目标是类似 AI 的 MRI——让我们「看进去」并理解正在发生什么的工具，类似于医学成像如何 revolutionize 对人体的理解

- **许多风险源于缺乏理解：** 多个风险类别——对齐失败、滥用、意外行为、权力寻求——因我们无法理解 AI 系统在做什么以及为何而加剧。可解释性可以解决根本原因

- **可行性 vs. 速度：** 文章谨慎乐观地认为可解释性可实现，但强调我们正处于与能力增长的竞赛中

## 重要引言

- 「我们仍能驾驭巴士——但时间不多了。」

- 「现代 AI 是生长的，不是构建的。我们不理解我们创造了什么。」

- 「目标类似 AI 的 MRI——一种看进去的方式。」

- 「我们的许多风险源于单一根本原因：我们不理解这些系统。」

- 「近期突破使可解释性 feasible。但 AI 进展更快。」

## 总结

可解释性文章代表了阿莫迪对为何理解 AI 内部重要最 focused 的陈述。通过将其 framing 为「urgent」而非 merely「important」，他提升了优先级。「 steering 巴士」隐喻抓住了时间维度：我们有一个窗口，它正在关闭。「生长而非构建」的定性解释了为何可解释性比传统软件更难——以及为何更必要。「AI 的 MRI」目标提供了 concrete enough 指导研究的 aspirational 目标。与多个风险类别（对齐、滥用等）的联系论证可解释性是一个 leverage 点——解决它可能解决许多问题。文章在个人渠道（而非 Anthropic）发表可能反映它代表他的个人观点与公司策略一样多，尽管 Anthropic 的可解释性团队（由 Chris Olah 领导）是其研究的主要部分。文章影响了后续关于 AI 安全优先级的讨论。

# The Urgency of AI Interpretability

**Date:** April 2025

**Platform/Venue:** Essay published on darioamodei.com and LessWrong

**Speaker:** Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic

**Duration:** Long-form essay

## Background/Context

"The Urgency of AI Interpretability" is an essay in which Dario Amodei argues that AI interpretability research has become critically urgent. Published on his personal website and LessWrong, the essay builds on earlier discussions of mechanistic interpretability (understanding how neural networks work internally) but frames it with greater urgency. The timing—April 2025—reflects his view that the window for "steering the bus" before AI becomes overwhelmingly powerful is closing. The essay connects interpretability to multiple risk categories and argues that recent breakthroughs have made the research feasible but that AI advances faster than interpretability progress.

## Key Points

- **Critical Urgency:** AI interpretability is not just important—it is critically urgent. The window to develop understanding before AI systems become overwhelmingly powerful is limited

- **"Steer the Bus" Window:** We can still "steer the bus" before AI becomes so powerful that we lose control. Interpretability is essential for steering—you need to understand a system to control it

- **Recent Breakthroughs:** Interpretability research has made significant progress. Recent breakthroughs have demonstrated that understanding neural network internals is feasible, not impossible

- **The Race:** AI advances faster than interpretability. Capabilities are growing rapidly while our understanding lags. This creates a dangerous gap

- **Modern AI is Fundamentally Opaque:** Unlike traditional software, modern AI is "grown" not "built." We don't explicitly program behavior; we train systems that develop emergent capabilities. This makes interpretability essential rather than optional

- **Goal: "MRI for AI":** The aspirational goal is something like an MRI for AI—a tool that lets us "see inside" and understand what's happening, similar to how medical imaging revolutionized understanding of the human body

- **Many Risks Stem from Lack of Understanding:** Multiple risk categories—alignment failures, misuse, unexpected behaviors, power-seeking—are exacerbated by our inability to understand what AI systems are doing and why. Interpretability could address root causes

- **Feasibility vs. Speed:** The essay is cautiously optimistic that interpretability is achievable but emphasizes that we're in a race against capability growth

## Notable Quotes

- "We can still steer the bus—but not for long."

- "Modern AI is grown, not built. We don't understand what we've created."

- "The goal is something like an MRI for AI—a way to see inside."

- "Many of our risks stem from a single root cause: we don't understand these systems."

- "Recent breakthroughs have made interpretability feasible. But AI advances faster."

## Summary

The interpretability essay represents Amodei's most focused statement on why understanding AI internals matters. By framing it as "urgent" rather than merely "important," he elevated the priority. The "steer the bus" metaphor captures the temporal dimension: we have a window, and it's closing. The "grown not built" characterization explains why interpretability is harder than for traditional software—and why it's more necessary. The "MRI for AI" goal provides an aspirational target that's concrete enough to guide research. The connection to multiple risk categories (alignment, misuse, etc.) argues that interpretability is a leverage point—solving it could address many problems. The essay's publication on personal channels (rather than Anthropic) may reflect that it represents his personal view as much as company strategy, though Anthropic's interpretability team (led by Chris Olah) is a major part of their research. The essay has influenced subsequent discussions of AI safety priorities.
