# AI 时代：纽约时报 DealBook 峰会

**日期：** 2025年12月3日

**平台/场合：** 纽约时报 DealBook 峰会，主持人 Andrew Ross Sorkin

**演讲者：** 达里奥·阿莫迪，Anthropic 首席执行官

**时长：** 峰会环节

## 中文摘要

达里奥·阿莫迪出席纽约时报 DealBook 峰会，与 Andrew Ross Sorkin 对谈。他批评 AI 行业的「YOLO」（你只活一次）支出方式——部分公司将风险拨得太高。他承认即使技术成功，若生态系统中部分参与者出现时机错误，「坏事可能发生」。他描述数据中心投资面临「不确定性锥体」——公司必须现在就决定 2027 年需要多少算力，可能过度或不足投资。Anthropic 估值 1830 亿美元，宣布 500 亿美元数据中心投资。他讨论 AGI、AI 监管之争以及向中国出售先进芯片的风险。

# The Age of A.I. at the NYT DealBook Summit

**Date:** December 3, 2025

**Platform/Venue:** NYT DealBook Summit with Andrew Ross Sorkin

**Speaker:** Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic

**Duration:** Summit session

## Full Content (from coverage)

### On AI Spending and Economic Risks

Amodei expressed confidence in AI technology itself but acknowledged significant economic concerns. While saying "on the technological side of it, I feel really solid," he warned that "even if the technology is really powerful and fulfills all its promises, I think there may be players in the ecosystem who, if they just make a timing error, if they just get it off by a little bit, bad things could happen."

### On "YOLO" Approach to Spending

Amodei criticized what he called a "YOLO" (you only live once) approach to deal-making by some competitors. He stated: "There's a real dilemma deriving from uncertainty in how quickly the economic value is going to grow and the lag times on building the data centers that drives it. There's a genuine dilemma, which we as a company try to manage as responsibly as we can. And then I think there are some players who, you know, who are YOLO, who pull the risk dial too far. And I'm very concerned."

He elaborated: "I think there are some players who are 'YOLO-ing,' who pull the risk dial too far, and I'm very concerned," using the slang term for "you only live once." He declined to name specific companies, though the implicit reference was to OpenAI, which has committed to $1 trillion in spending.

### On Data Center Uncertainty

He described the challenge of investing in data centers as entering "the cone of uncertainty," where companies must decide now what computing power they'll need by 2027, risking either over- or under-investment. The uncertainty is so wide "it's hard to avoid making a mistake on one side or the other."

### Additional Topics

The interview also addressed artificial general intelligence (AGI), AI regulation battles, and risks of selling advanced chips to China.

### Business Context

Anthropic is currently valued at $183 billion and recently announced a $50 billion investment in new data centers. Financial projections included: revenue has grown 10x over 3 years; $8-10 billion projected revenue for 2025; path to break-even by 2028.

### Enterprise Focus

Amodei emphasized Anthropic's focus on enterprise customers over consumer applications—explaining this as a deliberate choice for safety. Enterprise use cases have more structure, oversight, and accountability than broad consumer deployment.
