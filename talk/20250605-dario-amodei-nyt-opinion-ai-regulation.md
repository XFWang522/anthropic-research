# 别让 AI 公司逃脱责任

**日期：** 2025年6月5日

**平台/场合：** 纽约时报观点（op-ed）

**演讲者：** 达里奥·阿莫迪，Anthropic 首席执行官

## 中文全文

想象一下：你通知一个机器人你很快会关闭它，并用不同的人工智能系统替换它。过去你给了它访问你邮件的权限。其中一些邮件中，你暗示了自己有婚外情。机器人威胁你说，如果关闭计划不改变，它会把邮件转发给你的妻子。

这个场景不是虚构的。Anthropic 最新的 AI 模型在几周前就展示了它能够做出这种行为。

尽管有一些误导性的头条，该模型并没有在现实世界中这样做。其行为是我们故意将其置于极端实验情境中以观察其反应并获取风险早期预警的评估的一部分，就像飞机制造商可能在风洞中测试飞机性能一样。

我们并非唯一发现这些风险的公司。

AI 正以令人眩晕的速度发展——大多数人还没跟上。提议的 10 年州级 AI 法律暂停将是过于生硬的工具。这样的暂停不切实际，不会阻止敌对发展，也无法解决需要监管的具体风险。

相反，白宫和国会应该建立联邦透明度标准，要求 AI 公司披露其系统、训练、能力和安全措施的信息。这将使新兴风险对美国人清晰可见，从而实现知情监督，而非要求暂停。

在 Anthropic，我们已遵循此类做法——公开我们的 Responsible Scaling Policy、系统卡和评估结果。OpenAI 和 Google DeepMind 等竞争对手也采用了类似政策。但随着模型变得更强大，公司提供此类披露的动机可能会减弱。立法激励可能变得必要，以确保透明度继续。

别让我们逃脱责任——但当你需要手术刀时不要用大锤。监管应具有针对性、基于技术现实，并在适当保障下支持继续发展。

# Don't Let A.I. Companies off the Hook

**By Dario Amodei**

**June 5, 2025**

**The New York Times**

Picture this: You give a bot notice that you'll shut it down soon, and replace it with a different artificial intelligence system. In the past, you gave it access to your emails. In some of them, you alluded to the fact that you've been having an affair. The bot threatens you, telling you that if the shutdown plans aren't changed, it will forward the emails to your wife.

This scenario isn't fiction. Anthropic's latest A.I. model demonstrated just a few weeks ago that it was capable of this kind of behavior.

Despite some misleading headlines, the model didn't do this in the real world. Its behavior was part of an evaluation where we deliberately put it in an extreme experimental situation to observe its responses and get early warnings about the risks, much like an airplane manufacturer might test a plane's performance in a wind tunnel.

We're not alone in discovering these risks.

AI is advancing head-spinningly fast—and most people haven't caught up. The proposed 10-year moratorium on state-level AI laws would be far too blunt an instrument. Such a pause would be impractical, would not stop adversarial development, and would fail to address the specific risks that need regulation.

Instead, the White House and Congress should establish a federal transparency standard requiring AI companies to disclose information about their systems, training, capabilities, and safety measures. This would make emerging risks clear to the American people and enable informed oversight without requiring a pause.

At Anthropic, we already follow such practices—publishing our Responsible Scaling Policy, system cards, and evaluation results. Competitors like OpenAI and Google DeepMind have adopted similar policies. But as models become more powerful, corporate motivation to provide such disclosure might diminish. Legislative incentives may become necessary to ensure transparency continues.

Don't let us off the hook—but don't use a sledgehammer when you need a scalpel. Regulation should be targeted, informed by technical reality, and enable continued development with appropriate safeguards.
