## 概述

Sam McCandlish 在 LessWrong 上澄清了 Anthropic 对非贬损条款的立场，回应了「OpenAI 非贬损金丝雀」等帖子引发的行业审查。他表示 Anthropic 从未将非贬损与已归属股权挂钩，公司自 2024 年 6 月 1 日起从标准协议中移除此类条款，签署者可自由声明自己签署过，且 Anthropic 不会因安全关切执行非贬损。McCandlish 将此置于 Anthropic 偏离标准企业实践、与 AI 安全使命一致的更广泛承诺中，援引长期利益信托（LTBT）和负责任扩展政策（RSP）为例。

---

## 要点

### 从未与已归属股权挂钩

- Anthropic 未使用会因发声而没收股权的「金手铐」
- 与某些公司不同，已归属股权从未以非贬损为条件
- 员工可以离开并发声而无需财务惩罚

### 从标准协议中移除

- 自 2024 年 6 月 1 日起，Anthropic 从标准协议中移除非贬损
- 回应行业批评的主动变革
- 与公司透明和安全价值观一致

### 不会因安全关切执行

- 即使存在非贬损，Anthropic 也不会因安全相关言论执行
- 应提出安全关切——公司不想压制举报人
- 承诺允许员工谈论风险

### 行业背景

- 2024 年非贬损条款受到审查
- 批评者认为可能压制安全研究人员
- Anthropic 的立场与部分竞争对手区分
- 更广泛问责和透明运动的一部分

## Summary

Sam McCandlish clarified Anthropic's position on non-disparagement clauses in comments on LessWrong, responding to posts such as "Non-Disparagement Canaries for OpenAI" which documented OpenAI's practice of tying non-disparagement to vested equity. McCandlish stated that Anthropic never tied non-disparagement to vested equity, that the company was removing such clauses from standard agreements since June 1, 2024, and that Anthropic would not enforce non-disparagement for safety concerns. He framed this as part of Anthropic's broader commitment to diverge from standard corporate practices to align with their AI safety mission, citing the Long Term Benefit Trust and Responsible Scaling Policy as examples.

---

## Full Statement (Key Points from McCandlish's Comment)

1. **Equity ties denied**: Anthropic has never tied non-disparagement agreements to vested equity. Employees never risked losing vested equity for criticizing the company—unlike practices documented at other labs.

2. **Historical practice**: The company historically included standard non-disparagement terms in severance agreements and some non-US employment contracts. The company recognized this conflicted with their mission and began removing these terms from standard agreements as of June 1, 2024.

3. **Freedom to disclose**: Anyone who signed a non-disparagement agreement with Anthropic is free to state that fact. McCandlish acknowledged that previous agreements were unclear on this point and expressed regret.

4. **Safety concerns welcomed**: If someone who signed such an agreement wants to raise safety concerns at Anthropic, the company welcomes this feedback and will not enforce the non-disparagement agreement against them. Anthropic maintains an anonymous RSP non-compliance reporting line for employees to raise concerns without fear of retaliation.

5. **Broader commitment**: McCandlish framed this as part of Anthropic's mission to ensure AI goes well, noting that "standard corporate best practices won't cut it when the stakes are this high" and emphasizing their goal to set new standards for governance in AI development. He cited the Long Term Benefit Trust and Responsible Scaling Policy as other examples of Anthropic diverging from standard corporate practice.

---

## Key Points

### Never Tied to Vested Equity

- Anthropic did not use "golden handcuffs" that would forfeit equity for speaking out
- Unlike some companies, vested equity was never conditional on non-disparagement
- Employees could leave and speak without financial penalty

### Removing from Standard Agreements

- As of June 1, 2024, Anthropic was removing non-disparagement from standard agreements
- Proactive change in response to industry criticism
- Aligns with company values of transparency and safety

### Will Not Enforce for Safety Concerns

- Even where non-disparagement existed, Anthropic would not enforce it for safety-related speech
- Safety concerns should be raised—company does not want to silence whistleblowers
- Commitment to allowing employees to speak about risks

### Industry Context

- Non-disparagement clauses came under scrutiny in 2024
- Critics argued they could silence safety researchers
- Anthropic's position differentiated it from some competitors
- Part of broader accountability and transparency movement
