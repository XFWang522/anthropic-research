## 概述

## Summary

Mrinank Sharma, Anthropic's former Head of Safeguards Research, resigned with a public statement that "the world is in peril" and that he faced "pressures to set aside what matters most" within the organization. He announced plans to return to the UK and pursue poetry. The resignation raised questions about internal tensions at Anthropic between commercial pressures and safety priorities.

---

### "The World is in Peril"

- Sharma's framing of the current moment
- AI development poses existential and near-term risks
- Urgency of safety work
- Personal sense of responsibility

### "Pressures to Set Aside What Matters Most"

- Suggestion of internal tension at Anthropic
- Commercial or product pressures potentially conflicting with safety
- Implicit criticism of organizational direction
- Resonated with broader industry concerns

### Head of Safeguards Research

- Sharma led research on AI safeguards—safety measures, evaluations, mitigations
- Key role in Anthropic's safety infrastructure
- Resignation of a senior safety lead is notable
- Succession and continuity questions

### Plans: Return to UK, Poetry

- Sharma announced personal plans post-resignation
- Return to UK (he may have been based in US)
- Poetry as a creative outlet
- Suggests stepping back from AI industry

### Industry Context

- Resignations of safety researchers have drawn attention in 2024-2026
- Questions about whether labs can balance commercial and safety goals
- Anthropic had been seen as more safety-focused—resignation complicated that narrative
- Ongoing debate about organizational incentives
