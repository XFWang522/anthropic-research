# 达里奥·阿莫迪接受 CBS 新闻采访：五角大楼冲突

**日期：** 2026年2月27日

**平台/场合：** CBS 新闻独家采访，记者 Jo Ling Kent

**演讲者：** 达里奥·阿莫迪，Anthropic 首席执行官

**时长：** 新闻片段/采访

## 背景/语境

达里奥·阿莫迪在国防部宣布 Anthropic 为「国家安全供应链风险」数小时后接受了 CBS 新闻记者 Jo Ling Kent 的独家采访。该宣布实际上禁止 Anthropic 参与联邦合同，标志着自 Anthropic 拒绝国防部长最后通牒以来冲突的戏剧性升级。时间——在 Anthropic 发布解释其立场的博客文章第二天——使这次采访成为对政府行动的快速回应。

## 要点

- **国防部宣布：** 采访前数小时，国防部宣布 Anthropic 为「国家安全供应链风险」——这一指定会触发联邦采购排除

- **特朗普政府回应：** 特朗普政府因 Anthropic 的拒绝而禁止其参与联邦合同

- **拒绝的讨论：** 阿莫迪解释了 Anthropic 为何拒绝国防部长的要求，重申了两条红线

- **两条红线：** 重申不可协商的立场：1. 不开发完全自主武器 2. 不进行大规模国内监控

- **原则高于商业：** 将决定框定为原则问题——Anthropic 愿意接受商业后果（失去联邦合同）也不越过其认为不可接受的底线

- **国家安全立场：** 主张 Anthropic 的立场服务于长期国家安全——自主武器和大规模监控可能损害民主价值观，创造的风险超过短期能力收益

- **对「供应链风险」的回应：** 回应国防部的定性，可能主张该指定是对 Anthropic 拒绝的回应，而非对风险的客观评估

## 重要引言

- 「几小时前他们宣布我们是供应链风险。我们说不行时就知道这可能发生。」

- 「两条红线。我们不能越过。」

- 「我们宁愿失去合同，也不愿构建他们要求的东西。」

- 「这不是关于回避政府工作。而是关于我们愿意构建什么。」

- 「我们认为我们的立场从长远来看服务于国家安全。」

## 总结

CBS 新闻采访是阿莫迪对国防部宣布的首次公开回应。快速的时间——宣布后数小时——表明 Anthropic 已准备好解释和捍卫其立场。采访强化了两条红线和拒绝的原则性。阿莫迪的 framing 表明，失去联邦合同是维护价值观的可接受代价。他关于该立场服务于长期国家安全的论点重新框定了冲突：不是公司与政府对立，而是对什么服务于国家安全的不同看法。特朗普政府的禁令增添了政治语境。CBS 新闻这一主流广播平台意味着故事触达了广泛受众。采访确立了 Anthropic 的叙事：他们划定了清晰的界限，政府以排除作为回应，Anthropic 坚持其决定。

# Dario Amodei on CBS News: Pentagon Conflict

**Date:** February 27, 2026

**Platform/Venue:** CBS News exclusive with Jo Ling Kent

**Speaker:** Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic

**Duration:** News segment / interview

## Background/Context

Dario Amodei gave an exclusive interview to CBS News correspondent Jo Ling Kent, conducted hours after the Department of Defense declared Anthropic a "supply chain risk to national security." The declaration effectively banned Anthropic from federal contracts and represented a dramatic escalation of the conflict that had begun with Anthropic's refusal to accede to the Defense Secretary's ultimatum. The timing—the day after Anthropic's blog post explaining its position—made this a rapid-response interview to the government's action.

## Key Points

- **DoD Declaration:** Hours before the interview, the Department of Defense had declared Anthropic a "supply chain risk to national security"—a designation that triggers exclusion from federal contracting

- **Trump Administration Response:** The Trump administration had banned Anthropic from federal contracts in response to the company's refusal

- **Discussion of Refusal:** Amodei explained why Anthropic had refused the Defense Secretary's demands, reiterating the two red lines

- **Two Red Lines:** Reiterated the non-negotiable positions:
  1. No fully autonomous weapons
  2. No mass domestic surveillance

- **Principle Over Business:** Framed the decision as a matter of principle—Anthropic was willing to accept the business consequences (loss of federal contracts) rather than cross lines it considered unacceptable

- **National Security Stance:** Argued that Anthropic's position served long-term national security—that autonomous weapons and mass surveillance could undermine democratic values and create risks that outweigh short-term capability gains

- **Response to "Supply Chain Risk":** Addressed the DoD's characterization, likely arguing that the designation was a response to Anthropic's refusal rather than an objective assessment of risk

## Notable Quotes

- "Hours ago they declared us a supply chain risk. We knew this was possible when we said no."

- "Two red lines. We couldn't cross them."

- "We'd rather lose the contracts than build what they asked for."

- "This isn't about avoiding government work. It's about what we're willing to build."

- "We think our position serves national security in the long run."

## Summary

The CBS News interview was Amodei's first public response to the DoD's declaration. The rapid timing—hours after the announcement—showed Anthropic was prepared to explain and defend its position. The interview reinforced the two red lines and the principled nature of the refusal. Amodei's framing suggested that losing federal contracts was an acceptable cost for maintaining values. His argument that the position serves long-term national security reframed the conflict: not as a company versus government, but as different views of what serves national security. The Trump administration's ban added political context. The CBS News venue—mainstream broadcast—meant the story reached a broad audience. The interview established Anthropic's narrative: they had drawn clear lines, the government had responded with exclusion, and Anthropic was standing by its decision.
