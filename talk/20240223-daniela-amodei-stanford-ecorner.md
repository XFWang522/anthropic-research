## 概述

Daniela Amodei 在斯坦福 eCorner 与 Emily Ma 进行 53 分钟对话，讨论 Anthropic 七位联合创始人如何从第一天起将价值观与伦理护栏融入 AI 工具。涵盖宪法 AI、公益公司结构、AI 治理、可解释性研究、Claude 2 应用及 Claude 3 愿景。

---

## 要点摘要（中文）

- **七位联合创始人**：与兄弟 Dario 及另外五人共同离开 OpenAI 创立 Anthropic；Dario 负责技术愿景，Daniela 负责运营与商业。
- **宪法 AI**：以联合国人权宣言等约 17 份全球文献为基础，赋予 Claude 一套「宪法」指导其行为；集体宪法 AI 则通过大规模、人口多元的民意调查调整伦理输入。
- **公益公司**：Anthropic 为公益公司，认为不应由单一公司决定技术走向；政策团队自成立即有，与政府、公民社会、非营利组织合作。
- **机制可解释性**：类比神经科学对大脑的研究，旨在理解神经网络中哪些神经元在激活、是否存在问题性组合。
- **Claude 应用**：保险、医疗、法律等传统行业早期采用；Claude 幻觉率市场最低；医疗场景中与医生协作总结诊疗记录。

## Summary

Daniela Amodei spoke with Emily Ma for 53 minutes about how Anthropic's seven co-founders built values and ethical guardrails into their AI tools from day one. Topics included Constitutional AI, public benefit corporation structure, AI governance, mechanistic interpretability, Claude 2 applications, and Claude 3 vision.

---

## Key Transcript Excerpts

### Co-Founding with Sibling and Team

**Daniela Amodei:** "To make it even more complicated than just being a co-founder with my brother, I actually have five other co-founders. So there were seven of us total who left OpenAI together. We all co-founded Anthropic... Dario and I worked the most closely together of the co-founders as CEO and president... What has worked especially well for us is just a ton of clarity on what are our zones of kind of ownership, competence and genius."

**Daniela Amodei:** "Dario is the kind of technical... He really is thinking about what is the five year arc of what's happening in generative AI? So much of the work that he and the team did at OpenAI was building GPT-2, GPT-3, but also a ton of seminal work in technical safety research... I'm much more sort of practical, the business oriented side... how to actually grow and scale the company of Anthropic and then also what's happening on the product and business side."

### Anthropic's Mission

**Daniela Amodei:** "Anthropic is aiming to build transformative general AI systems that are safe, reliable, steerable, ethical... So much of why we were founded was this desire to build generative AI tools and systems and products that people could use while feeling really, really confident that what we were putting on the market was trustworthy, reliable, and safe... We have this sort of belief that incorporating these technical safety streams into training of the model really from day one is the best way to ensure that when they actually are in a product form and getting into the hands of customers, that they're gonna be safe."

### Constitutional AI

**Daniela Amodei:** "The way that you used to make sure they're not saying nasty things or replicating bad inherent biases was reinforcement learning from human feedback... But what we found was that a lot of subtler things about how models respond, react, things that they're deeply believing are much harder to train out in those individual cases. And so we had this idea of giving the model kind of a broader constitution... We shouldn't necessarily be the arbiters of what is good or bad... Let's think about what broader documents that already exist in the world that have grappled with some of these challenges, and incorporate—I think we have something like 17 different founding documents in the constitution."

**Daniela Amodei:** "We recently did a set of research called collective constitutional AI. Instead of using just a set of founding documents, we actually polled a very large, demographically diverse group of people from around the country and in other parts of the world to see how did they react to some of the things that were in our constitution and what did they collectively come to on some of these ethical questions?"

### Governing AI Collaboratively

**Daniela Amodei:** "Anthropic is a company. We're a public benefit corporation and we have this very lofty social mission. And I don't think anybody, including us, thinks it's right for us or any particular company alone to be the arbiter of what happens with this technology... We've had a policy team basically since day one... We also work with a number of civil society and nonprofit groups... those groups often have additional expertise that we don't necessarily have within our walls."

### Mechanistic Interpretability

**Daniela Amodei:** "A research team at Anthropic in the area of mechanistic interpretability... one of my other co-founders sort of pioneered, first at Google actually, and then at OpenAI and now at Anthropic. Really the best way to think of mechanistic interpretability is almost as the equivalent of neuroscience and what neuroscientists do to the human brain, what mechanistic interpretability experts do to neural networks... When these neural networks are producing outputs, we don't know what's happening... If we could actually go in and say, what are the literal neurons that are firing that are causing the model to think this or do this? And are there combinations that are maybe problematic that are firing together?"

### Claude Applications

**Daniela Amodei:** "Very large enterprise businesses have been some of our earliest adopters... More traditional industries like insurance, healthcare, legal services—these are the types of industries that really value reliability, trustworthiness with their end customers. And where things like lower hallucination rates are actually a huge deal for them. Claude has the lowest hallucination rate on market."

**Daniela Amodei:** "In the healthcare space, Claude can be a great partner in concert with a medical practitioner... summarizing key findings from a health consult... Claude is a great partner for helping them to not do as much paperwork."

### Claude 3 Vision

**Daniela Amodei:** "For Claude 3, number one, on some of the core safety features—we have this helpful, honest, harmless framework. We're hoping to just make improvements on all of those. A huge one is, the number one request we hear from model quality from our customers is how do you get the hallucination rate from X percent to 0%?"
