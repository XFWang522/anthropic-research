## 完整内容

### 与特朗普政府监管观点的分歧

特朗普政府可能认为监管正在扼杀 AI 行业，但行业最大的参与者之一并不同意。在 WIRED 2025 年 12 月 4 日于旧金山举行的 Big Interview 活动上，Anthropic 总裁兼联合创始人 Daniela Amodei 与特约编辑 Steven Levy 对话。尽管特朗普的 AI 和加密沙皇 David Sacks 可能发推称她的公司「基于恐惧营销运行复杂的监管捕获策略」，Amodei 坚信公司对指出 AI 潜在危险的承诺正在使行业更强大。

### 市场将奖励安全的 AI

Amodei 表示：「我们从第一天起就非常直言不讳，我们觉得 AI 有这种不可思议的潜力。我们真的希望让整个世界实现 AI 的潜力、积极效益和好处，要做到这一点，我们必须把困难的事情做对。我们必须让风险可控。这就是为什么我们如此频繁地谈论它。」

超过 30 万家初创公司、开发者和公司使用 Anthropic 的 Claude 模型的某种版本。Amodei 表示，通过与这些品牌的接触，她了解到，虽然客户希望 AI 能做出伟大的事情，但他们也希望它可靠且安全。

### 没有人想要「更不安全」的产品

Amodei 说：「没有人说『我们想要更不安全的产品』。」她将 Anthropic 报告其模型限制和越狱比作汽车公司发布碰撞测试研究，以展示如何解决安全问题。在视频中看到碰撞假人飞出车窗可能令人震惊，但得知汽车制造商因此更新了车辆安全功能可能会让买家购买那辆车。Amodei 表示，使用 Anthropic AI 产品的公司也是如此，这形成了一个某种程度上自我监管的市场。

### 设定最低安全标准

Amodei 说：「我们正在设定的几乎可以理解为最低安全标准，仅仅通过我们投入经济中的东西。公司现在正在围绕 AI 构建许多工作流程和日常工具任务，他们就像，『嗯，我们知道这个产品幻觉不那么严重，不产生有害内容，不做所有这些坏事。』你为什么要选择在那些方面得分较低的竞争对手？」

### 宪法式 AI

Amodei 讨论了 Anthropic 对所谓「宪法式 AI」的承诺，即用一套基线伦理原则和教授人类价值观的文档来训练模型。使用联合国世界人权宣言等来训练模型，Amodei 说，可以快速教 LLM 如何根据整体伦理意义上的是非来回应查询，而不是根据查询在经验上是对是错、是好是坏。

### 人才与使命

Amodei 表示，Anthropic 对创造更好、更伦理的 AI 模型的承诺也帮助其留住人才。「我们从进门的人那里听到的故事是，使命、价值观以及这种对好坏都诚实的渴望、帮助让坏事变好的渴望，感觉非常真实，就像我们是认真的，」她解释。

### 增长与扩展定律

也许这就是为什么 Anthropic 在过去几年里 staff 突飞猛进，从 200 名员工增长到超过 2000 人。虽然这些数字在考虑所有 AI 泡沫论时可能看起来令人担忧，但 Amodei 表示她没有看到公司或行业放缓的任何迹象。

「根据我们看到的，模型继续以扩展定律所谈论的那种曲线变聪明，收入也继续在那条曲线上，」Amodei 说。「正如在 Anthropic 工作的任何科学家会告诉你的，一切都在曲线上继续，直到它不继续，所以我们真的努力对此保持自我意识和谦逊。」

### 核心论点

- **市场将奖励安全的 AI**：企业和消费者将偏好可信的 AI
- **安全是竞争优势**：不是成本中心
- **宪法式 AI**：用联合国宣言等原则训练模型
- **透明度建立信任**：报告限制和越狱如同汽车碰撞测试
- **使命吸引人才**：员工被诚实对待好坏的态度吸引
- **监管不会扼杀**：明智的监管可以促进创新

## Full Content

### Disagreement with Trump Administration on Regulation

The Trump administration may think regulation is crippling the AI industry, but one of the industry's biggest players doesn't agree. At WIRED's Big Interview event on Thursday, December 4, 2025, in San Francisco, Anthropic president and cofounder Daniela Amodei told editor at large Steven Levy that even though Trump's AI and crypto czar David Sacks may have tweeted that her company is "running a sophisticated regulatory capture strategy based on fear-mongering," she's convinced her company's commitment to calling out the potential dangers of AI is making the industry stronger.

### The Market Will Reward Safe AI

Amodei said: "We were very vocal from day one that we felt there was this incredible potential [for AI]. We really want to be able to have the entire world realize the potential, the positive benefits, and the upside that can come from AI and in order to do that, we have to get the tough things right. We have to make the risks manageable. And that's why we talk about it so much."

Over 300,000 startups, developers, and companies use some version of Anthropic's Claude model. Amodei said that, through the company's dealings with those brands, she's learned that, while customers want their AI to be able to do great things, they also want it to be reliable and safe.

### No One Wants 'Less Safe' Product

Amodei said: "No one says 'we want a less safe product.'" She likened Anthropic's reporting of its model's limits and jailbreaks to that of a car company releasing crash-test studies to show how it's addressed safety concerns. It might seem shocking to see a crash test dummy flying through a car window in a video, but learning that an automaker updated their vehicle's safety features as a result of that test could sell a buyer on a car. Amodei said the same goes for companies using Anthropic's AI products, making for a market that is somewhat self-regulating.

### Setting Minimum Safety Standards

Amodei said: "We're setting what you can almost think of as minimum safety standards just by what we're putting into the economy. [Companies] are now building many workflows and day-to-day tooling tasks around AI, and they're like, 'Well, we know that this product doesn't hallucinate as much, it doesn't produce harmful content, and it doesn't do all of these bad things.' Why would you go with a competitor that is going to score lower on that?"

### Constitutional AI

Amodei discussed Anthropic's commitment to what it calls "Constitutional AI," where it trains its models on a baseline set of ethical principles and documents that teach human values. Using something like the United Nations Universal Declaration of Human Rights to train a model, Amodei said, can quickly teach a LLM how to respond to queries based not on the idea that a query is right or wrong, good or bad empirically, but rather than an issue is right or wrong in an overall ethical sense.

### Talent and Mission

Amodei said that Anthropic's commitment to creating a better, more ethical AI model has also helped it retain talent. "The story that we hear from people that come in the door [at Anthropic] is there's something about the mission and the values and this desire to be honest about both the good and the bad, and the desire to help to make the bad things better, that feels very genuine, like we mean it," she explained.

### Growth and Scaling Laws

Perhaps that's why Anthropic has grown its staff by leaps and bounds over the past few years, from 200 staffers to over 2,000. While those numbers could seem scary, especially when considering all the AI bubble talk flying around Wall Street and Silicon Valley, Amodei said she hasn't seen any sign of her company or industry slowing down.

"Based on what we're seeing, the models are continuing to get smarter at the exact sort of curve that the scaling laws talk about and the revenue is continuing on that same curve," Amodei said. "As any of the scientists that work at Anthropic would tell you, everything continues going on the curve until it doesn't and so we really try to be self aware and humble about that."

### Key Arguments

- **Market will reward safe AI**: Enterprises and consumers will prefer AI they can trust
- **Safety is competitive advantage**: Not a cost center
- **Constitutional AI**: Training models on principles like UN Declaration
- **Transparency builds trust**: Reporting limits and jailbreaks like car crash tests
- **Mission attracts talent**: Employees drawn to honest attitude about good and bad
- **Regulation won't cripple**: Smart regulation can foster innovation
