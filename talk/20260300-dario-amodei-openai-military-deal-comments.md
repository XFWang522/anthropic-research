# 达里奥·阿莫迪谈 OpenAI 军事合作：TechCrunch 报道

**日期：** 2026年3月

**平台/场合：** TechCrunch 报道（对 TechCrunch 的评论）

**演讲者：** 达里奥·阿莫迪，Anthropic 首席执行官

**时长：** 报道评论

## 背景/语境

TechCrunch 报道了达里奥·阿莫迪关于 OpenAI 围绕军事合作对外宣传的评论。该报道发布于 2026 年 3 月，正值 AI 公司的国防和政府合同受到更严格审查之际。OpenAI 已宣布或被报道达成重大军事或国防相关协议。阿莫迪的评论因其直率而引人注目——据报道，他称 OpenAI 的宣传为「彻头彻尾的谎言」。背景包括 Anthropic 自身与国防部在红线问题（自主武器、大规模监控）上的冲突，这可能影响了阿莫迪对 AI 公司应如何就军事工作与公众沟通的看法。

## 要点

- **OpenAI 军事合作：** OpenAI 已达成或正在寻求军事或国防相关协议。该合作的细节以及 OpenAI 的公开宣传是阿莫迪批评的对象

- **「彻头彻尾的谎言」：** 据报道，阿莫迪将 OpenAI 围绕军事合作的宣传定性为「彻头彻尾的谎言」——对竞争对手沟通方式的强烈而直接的批评

- **宣传与实质：** 批评似乎集中在 OpenAI 如何描述或包装该合作——其公开声明与实际协议性质之间的差距——而非（或除）合作本身的内容

- **Anthropic 立场的背景：** 阿莫迪的评论发生在 Anthropic 拒绝国防部要求并被禁止参与联邦合同之后。对比——Anthropic 划定红线并接受被排除，而 OpenAI 达成军事合作且（在阿莫迪看来）宣传具有误导性——可能促使了这番批评

- **行业动态：** 评论反映了 AI 公司在国防和政府工作上的紧张关系，各方采取不同方法、透明度也不同

- **TechCrunch 报道：** 该报道将阿莫迪的批评带给科技行业受众，TechCrunch 作为主要科技媒体的角色放大了这一故事

## 重要引言

- 「OpenAI 围绕军事合作的宣传是彻头彻尾的谎言。」（据 TechCrunch 报道）

## 总结

阿莫迪据称对 TechCrunch 的评论代表了他对竞争对手最直接的公开批评。将 OpenAI 的宣传称为「彻头彻尾的谎言」对一位 CEO 来说异常直率——竞争对手通常避免如此直接的攻击。背景很重要：Anthropic 刚因拒绝越过红线而被禁止参与联邦合同，而 OpenAI 则达成了军事合作。阿莫迪可能认为 OpenAI 对该合作的包装误导了公众，让他们对 OpenAI 实际在构建或同意什么产生误解。批评可能涉及：(1) 合作本身的内容（例如是否越过了 Anthropic 不会越过的红线）；(2) OpenAI 公开声明与实际协议之间的差距；或 (3) 暗示合作比实际更有限或更安全的包装。TechCrunch 的报道将争议带给了科技社区。这些评论强化了阿莫迪直率的声誉，以及他愿意批评他认为有问题的行业做法。

# Dario Amodei on OpenAI's Military Deal: TechCrunch Report

**Date:** March 2026

**Platform/Venue:** TechCrunch report (comments to TechCrunch)

**Speaker:** Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic

**Duration:** Reported comments

## Background/Context

TechCrunch reported on Dario Amodei's comments regarding OpenAI's messaging around a military deal. The report came in March 2026, in the context of increased scrutiny of AI companies' defense and government contracts. OpenAI had announced or been reported to have a significant military or defense-related agreement. Amodei's comments were notable for their directness—he reportedly called OpenAI's messaging "straight up lies." The context included Anthropic's own conflict with the DoD over red lines (autonomous weapons, mass surveillance), which may have informed Amodei's perspective on how AI companies should communicate about military work.

## Key Points

- **OpenAI Military Deal:** OpenAI had entered into or was pursuing a military or defense-related agreement. The details of the deal and OpenAI's public messaging about it were the subject of Amodei's criticism

- **"Straight Up Lies":** Amodei reportedly characterized OpenAI's messaging around the military deal as "straight up lies"—a strong and direct criticism of a competitor's communications

- **Messaging vs. Substance:** The criticism appeared to focus on how OpenAI was describing or framing the deal—the gap between their public statements and the actual nature of the agreement—rather than (or in addition to) the substance of the deal itself

- **Context of Anthropic's Position:** Amodei's comments came after Anthropic had refused DoD demands and been banned from federal contracts. The contrast—Anthropic drawing red lines and accepting exclusion, versus OpenAI's military deal and (in Amodei's view) misleading messaging—may have motivated the criticism

- **Industry Dynamics:** The comments reflected tension between AI companies on defense and government work, with different approaches and different levels of transparency

- **TechCrunch Coverage:** The report brought Amodei's criticism to a tech industry audience, with TechCrunch's role as a major tech publication amplifying the story

## Notable Quotes

- "OpenAI's messaging around the military deal is straight up lies." (as reported by TechCrunch)

- (Implied) Criticism of how AI companies communicate about sensitive government work

## Summary

Amodei's reported comments to TechCrunch represented his most direct public criticism of a competitor. Calling OpenAI's messaging "straight up lies" was unusually blunt for a CEO—typically competitors avoid such direct attacks. The context mattered: Anthropic had just been banned from federal contracts for refusing to cross red lines, while OpenAI had a military deal. Amodei may have seen OpenAI's framing of their deal as misleading the public about what they were actually building or agreeing to. The criticism could have been about: (1) the substance of the deal itself (e.g., whether it crossed lines Anthropic would not cross); (2) the gap between OpenAI's public statements and the actual agreement; or (3) the framing that suggested the deal was more limited or safer than it was. The TechCrunch report brought the controversy to the tech community. The comments reinforced Amodei's reputation for directness and his willingness to criticize industry practices he views as problematic.
