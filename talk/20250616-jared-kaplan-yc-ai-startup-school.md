## 概述

Jared Kaplan 在 Y Combinator AI 创业学院发表演讲，讨论扩展定律和 AI 能力的可预测增长。他认为「智能以可预测、近乎物理的方式扩展」，LLM 能力每 7 个月翻倍，AI「更像神经科学而非工程」。他建议创业公司为未来能力构建，而不仅是当前能力。

---

## 要点

### 可预测扩展

- "智能以可预测、近乎物理的方式扩展"
- 扩展定律描述性能如何随算力、数据和参数提升
- 非完美可预测，但趋势强劲
- 支持规划和投资决策

### LLM 每 7 个月翻倍

- 能力提升遵循大约翻倍的时间线
- 比芯片摩尔定律更快
- 暗示未来几年显著的能力提升
- 创业公司应据此规划

### 神经科学多于工程

- AI 开发「更像神经科学而非工程」
- 我们在发现智能如何运作，而非从第一性原理设计
- 经验观察和逆向工程
- 对我们理解的谦逊

### 为未来能力构建

- 不要只针对当今模型优化
- 预期 1-2 年后模型能做什么
- 架构和产品决策应考虑扩展
- 避免为将消失的局限构建

### 对创业公司的启示

- AI 创业公司有独特机遇——能力快速提升
- 时机重要——太早或太晚都有风险
- 与推动前沿的实验室合作
- 安全与能力必须同步推进

## Summary

Jared Kaplan spoke at Y Combinator's AI Startup School about scaling laws and the predictable growth of AI capabilities. He argued that "intelligence scales in a predictable, almost physical way," that LLMs are doubling capabilities every 7 months, and that AI "resembles neuroscience more than engineering." He advised startups to build for future capabilities, not just current ones.

---

## Key Points

### Predictable Scaling

- "Intelligence scales in a predictable, almost physical way"
- Scaling laws describe how performance improves with compute, data, and parameters
- Not perfectly predictable, but strong trends
- Enables planning and investment decisions

### LLMs Doubling Every 7 Months

- Capability improvements follow a rough doubling timeline
- Faster than Moore's Law for chips
- Implies significant capability gains in coming years
- Startups should plan for this trajectory

### Neuroscience More Than Engineering

- AI development "resembles neuroscience more than engineering"
- We're discovering how intelligence works, not designing it from first principles
- Empirical observation and reverse-engineering
- Humility about our understanding

### Build for Future Capabilities

- Don't optimize only for today's models
- Anticipate what models will be able to do in 1-2 years
- Architecture and product decisions should account for scaling
- Avoid building for limitations that will disappear

### Implications for Startups

- AI startups have a unique opportunity—capabilities are improving rapidly
- Timing matters—too early or too late both have risks
- Partner with labs that are pushing the frontier
- Safety and capability must advance together
