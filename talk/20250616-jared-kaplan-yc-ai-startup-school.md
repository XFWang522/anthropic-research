## 完整内容

### 可预测扩展

Jared Kaplan 在 Y Combinator 2025 年 6 月 16-17 日于旧金山举行的首届 AI 创业学院上发表演讲。他强调「智能以可预测、近乎物理的方式扩展」。扩展定律描述性能如何随算力、数据和参数提升。非完美可预测，但趋势强劲。支持规划和投资决策。Kaplan 从物理学背景出发，强调预训练和强化学习的扩展定律。

### LLM 每 7 个月翻倍

Kaplan 强调的一个关键发现是：**LLM 的任务能力大约每七个月翻倍**。能力提升遵循大约翻倍的时间线。比芯片摩尔定律更快。暗示未来几年显著的能力提升。创业公司应据此规划。

### 神经科学多于工程

Kaplan 指出机制可解释性存在显著差距，观察到当前方法更像神经科学而非工程。然而，他强调 LLM 提供了独特优势，因为它们产生大量可测量数据，使得逆向工程认知过程具有前所未有的机会。AI 开发「更像神经科学而非工程」。我们在发现智能如何运作，而非从第一性原理设计。经验观察和逆向工程。对我们理解的谦逊。

### 为未来能力构建

Kaplan 建议创始人构建产品时预期未来能力而非当前能力。这种前瞻方法反映了 AI 系统快速改进的轨迹。不要只针对当今模型优化。预期 1-2 年后模型能做什么。架构和产品决策应考虑扩展。避免为将消失的局限构建。

### 对创业公司的启示

AI 创业公司有独特机遇——能力快速提升。时机重要——太早或太晚都有风险。与推动前沿的实验室合作。安全与能力必须同步推进。

### 背景

Kaplan 从理论物理学学术生涯转型进入 AI，最终加入 Anthropic，专注于理解扩展如何驱动 AI 能力改进。他共同撰写了关于扩展定律的论文，该研究来自将物理学原理应用于 AI。

## Full Content

### Predictable Scaling

Jared Kaplan spoke at Y Combinator's inaugural AI Startup School on June 16-17, 2025 in San Francisco. He emphasized that "intelligence scales in a predictable, almost physical way." Scaling laws describe how performance improves with compute, data, and parameters. Not perfectly predictable, but strong trends. Enables planning and investment decisions. Kaplan drew from his physics background to emphasize the scaling laws of pretraining and reinforcement learning.

### LLMs Doubling Every 7 Months

A critical finding Kaplan highlighted is that **LLMs are doubling their task capabilities roughly every seven months**. Capability improvements follow a rough doubling timeline. Faster than Moore's Law for chips. Implies significant capability gains in coming years. Startups should plan for this trajectory.

### Neuroscience More Than Engineering

Kaplan noted significant gaps in mechanistic interpretability, observing that current approaches resemble neuroscience more than engineering. However, he emphasized that LLMs offer a unique advantage due to the extensive measurable data they generate, enabling unprecedented opportunities for reverse-engineering cognitive processes. AI development "resembles neuroscience more than engineering." We're discovering how intelligence works, not designing it from first principles. Empirical observation and reverse-engineering. Humility about our understanding.

### Build for Future Capabilities

Kaplan advised founders to build products anticipating future capabilities rather than current ones. This forward-looking approach reflects the rapid improvement trajectory of AI systems. Don't optimize only for today's models. Anticipate what models will be able to do in 1-2 years. Architecture and product decisions should account for scaling. Avoid building for limitations that will disappear.

### Implications for Startups

AI startups have a unique opportunity—capabilities are improving rapidly. Timing matters—too early or too late both have risks. Partner with labs that are pushing the frontier. Safety and capability must advance together.

### Background

Kaplan transitioned to AI from a career as a theoretical physicist working in academia, eventually joining Anthropic where he now focuses on understanding how scaling drives improvements in AI capabilities. He co-authored the paper on scaling laws, research that came from applying physics principles to AI.
