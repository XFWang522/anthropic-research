# 如果达里奥·阿莫迪关于 AI 的看法是对的怎么办？

**日期：** 2024年4月12日

**平台/场合：** The Ezra Klein Show（纽约时报）

**演讲者：** 达里奥·阿莫迪，Anthropic 首席执行官

**时长：** 约 1 小时 32 分钟

## 背景/语境

达里奥·阿莫迪做客 The Ezra Klein Show，这是纽约时报专栏作家 Ezra Klein 主持的最具影响力的政策和思想播客之一。节目标题「如果达里奥·阿莫迪关于 AI 的看法是对的怎么办？」将对话 framing 为认真对待阿莫迪观点的含义——suggesting 他的预测可能是正确的，但常被 dismiss 或 downplay。Klein 以 rigorous、哲学性的采访著称，探索技术和政治发展的 deeper 影响。露面时 AI 能力继续 rapid 进展，政策辩论加剧。

## 要点

- **AI 处于指数增长曲线：** 阿莫迪解释 AI 发展遵循指数轨迹，改进在多个维度上 compound。这创造了能力可能 surprise 甚至专家的动态

- **2–5 年内出现先进 AI：** 给出了 significantly 更先进 AI 系统何时到来的 concrete 时间线——2–5 年内——对社会需要多 urgently 准备有影响

- **权力与决策：** 提出了 striking 论点：AI 系统将变得如此 powerful，以至于其构建者不应被信任 unilateral 决定其使用方式。这是来自领先 AI 公司 CEO 的 significant 陈述，承认企业治理的局限

- **需要更广泛的治理：** 论证关于 AI 部署、用例和安全阈值的决策需要涉及政府、公民社会和公众——而不仅仅是构建系统的公司

- **为转型做准备：** 讨论了社会应如何为高度 capable AI 的到来做准备——教育、社会安全网、治理结构和国际协调

- **不确定性与谦逊：** 承认预测的不确定性，同时论证方向足够清晰，值得认真准备

## 重要引言

- 「系统将变得如此 powerful，以至于他和其他构建 AI 的人不应被信任 unilateral 决定其使用方式。」

- 关于指数增长：「我们处于大多数人尚未 internalize 的曲线上。」

- 关于治理：「构建这个的公司不应该是唯一决定其用途的。」

## 总结

The Ezra Klein Show 露面因阿莫迪愿意论证反对自己的权力而 notable。他关于 AI 构建者不应 unilateral 决定系统使用方式的陈述是来自 CEO 的 striking 让步——essentially 论证对其自身决策权的约束。节目标题邀请听众认真对待他的时间线（2–5 年先进 AI）并考虑其影响。Klein 的哲学方法引出了治理和政治维度：如果阿莫迪关于时间线和这些系统 power 的看法是对的，这对民主、谁有权做决定以及我们如何准备意味着什么？对话将阿莫迪定位为倡导更广泛社会 engagement AI 治理的人，而不仅仅是企业自我监管。它也突出了他作为构建者的角色与他对构建者权威局限的看法之间的 tension。

# What if Dario Amodei Is Right About A.I.?

**Date:** April 12, 2024

**Platform/Venue:** The Ezra Klein Show (The New York Times)

**Speaker:** Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic

**Duration:** Approximately 1 hour 32 minutes

## Background/Context

Dario Amodei appeared on The Ezra Klein Show, one of the most influential policy and ideas podcasts, hosted by New York Times columnist Ezra Klein. The episode title "What if Dario Amodei Is Right About A.I.?" framed the conversation around the implications of taking Amodei's views seriously—suggesting his predictions might be correct but are often dismissed or downplayed. Klein is known for rigorous, philosophical interviews that explore the deeper implications of technological and political developments. The appearance came as AI capabilities continued to advance rapidly and policy debates intensified.

## Key Points

- **AI on Exponential Growth Curve:** Amodei explained that AI development is following an exponential trajectory, with improvements compounding across multiple dimensions. This creates a dynamic where capabilities can surprise even experts

- **Advanced AI Arriving in 2-5 Years:** Gave a concrete timeline for when significantly more advanced AI systems would arrive—within 2-5 years—with implications for how urgently society needs to prepare

- **Power and Decision-Making:** Made a striking argument that AI systems will become so powerful that their builders should not be trusted to unilaterally decide how they're used. This was a significant statement from a CEO of a leading AI company, acknowledging the limits of corporate governance

- **Need for Broader Governance:** Argued that decisions about AI deployment, use cases, and safety thresholds need to involve governments, civil society, and the public—not just the companies building the systems

- **Preparing for Transition:** Discussed what society should do to prepare for the arrival of highly capable AI—education, social safety nets, governance structures, and international coordination

- **Uncertainty and Humility:** Acknowledged uncertainty in predictions while arguing that the direction of travel is clear enough to warrant serious preparation

## Notable Quotes

- "Systems will become so powerful that he and others building AI should not be trusted to unilaterally decide how they're used."

- On exponential growth: "We're on a curve that most people haven't internalized."

- On governance: "The companies building this shouldn't be the only ones deciding what happens with it."

## Summary

The Ezra Klein Show appearance was notable for Amodei's willingness to argue against his own power. His statement that AI builders should not unilaterally decide how systems are used was a striking concession from a CEO—essentially arguing for constraints on his own decision-making authority. The episode title invited listeners to take his timeline (2-5 years for advanced AI) seriously and consider the implications. Klein's philosophical approach drew out the governance and political dimensions: if Amodei is right about the timeline and the power of these systems, what does that mean for democracy, for who gets to make decisions, and for how we prepare? The conversation positioned Amodei as someone advocating for broader societal engagement with AI governance, not just corporate self-regulation. It also highlighted the tension between his role as a builder and his views about the limits of builder authority.
