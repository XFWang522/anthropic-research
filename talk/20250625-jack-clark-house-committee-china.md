## 概述

Jack Clark 在美国众议院中国共产党特别委员会作证，主张美国能赢得 AI 竞赛。他预测强大 AI 将于 2026 年底或 2027 年初到来，民主国家中的 AI 因开放性和安全干预而更优，中国模型缺乏可比的安全措施。他主张维持半导体出口管制。

---

## 要点

### 美国能赢

- Clark 表达美国能领先 AI 的信心
- 优势：人才、资本、生态、民主价值观
- 非必然——需要持续投入和政策
- 半导体和算力优势显著

### 2026 年底/2027 年初强大 AI

- 变革性 AI 能力的时间线预测
- 基于扩展趋势和行业轨迹
- 暗示政策和安全准备的紧迫性
- 非精确预测——合理范围

### 民主国家中的 AI 更优

- 民主社会在 AI 开发上有优势
- 开放性促进协作和审查
- 问责体系中更可能进行安全干预
- 价值观对齐对 AI 部署重要

### 中国模型缺乏安全干预

- 中国 AI 实验室有不同的激励和约束
- 透明度较低、公开安全承诺较少
- 有能力但无安全的地缘政治影响
- 出口管制作为杠杆

### 维持半导体出口管制

- 先进芯片是 AI 的关键瓶颈
- 出口管制限制中国获得尖端算力
- 更广泛技术竞争的一部分
- 经济利益与战略优势之间的平衡

## Summary

Jack Clark testified before the U.S. House Select Committee on the Chinese Communist Party, arguing that the US can win the AI race. He projected that powerful AI would arrive by late 2026 or early 2027, that AI in democracies is superior due to openness and safety interventions, and that Chinese models lack comparable safety measures. He advocated maintaining semiconductor export controls.

---

## Key Points

### US Can Win

- Clark expressed confidence that the US can lead in AI
- Advantages: talent, capital, ecosystem, democratic values
- Not inevitable—requires sustained investment and policy
- Semiconductor and compute advantage is significant

### Powerful AI by Late 2026/Early 2027

- Timeline projection for transformative AI capabilities
- Based on scaling trends and industry trajectory
- Implies urgency for policy and safety preparation
- Not a precise prediction—a plausible range

### AI in Democracies Superior

- Democratic societies have advantages for AI development
- Openness enables collaboration and scrutiny
- Safety interventions are more likely in accountable systems
- Values alignment matters for AI deployment

### Chinese Models Lack Safety Interventions

- Chinese AI labs have different incentives and constraints
- Less transparency, fewer public safety commitments
- Geopolitical implications of capability without safety
- Export controls as a lever

### Maintain Semiconductor Export Controls

- Advanced chips are a key bottleneck for AI
- Export controls limit Chinese access to cutting-edge compute
- Part of broader technology competition
- Balance between economic interests and strategic advantage
