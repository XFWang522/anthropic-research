## 概述

Jack Clark 在联合国安理会发表讲话，阐述 AI 治理需要强大的政府能力。他认为 AI 开发不能仅由私营部门主导，政府必须建立真正的技术和制度能力来理解和监管 AI，安全与权力集中本质上是相互关联的。他的证词强调需要"政府中真正扎实的能力"来监督 AI 开发。

---

## 要点

### 私营部门无法单独治理

- AI 开发影响重大，不能仅由行业治理
- 私营公司的激励可能与公共利益不一致
- 自我监管有局限——需要外部监督
- 政府必须是积极参与者，而非被动观察者

### 政府必须建立能力

- 许多政府缺乏评估 AI 系统的技术专长
- 需要"政府中真正扎实的能力"——不仅是咨询小组
- 能力包括：技术人才、评估基础设施、监管框架
- 政府 AI 专长投入刻不容缓

### 安全与权力集中相关联

- 随着 AI 变得更强大，能力集中在少数人手中会增加风险
- 大规模安全失败可能造成灾难性后果
- 治理必须同时解决技术安全和地缘政治动态
- 普及 AI 专长有助于分散风险

### 国际合作

- AI 是全球性技术——治理必须是国际性的
- 联合国安理会作为高层讨论平台
- 需要共同标准、信息共享和协调行动
- 避免在安全标准上竞相降低

## Summary

Jack Clark addressed the United Nations Security Council on the need for robust government capacity in AI governance. He argued that AI development cannot be left solely to the private sector, that governments must build real technical and institutional capacity to understand and regulate AI, and that safety and power concentration are inherently linked. His testimony emphasized the need for "real hard capacity in government" to oversee AI development.

---

## Key Points

### Private Sector Cannot Govern Alone

- AI development is too consequential to be governed only by industry
- Private companies have incentives that may not align with public interest
- Self-regulation has limits—external oversight is necessary
- Governments must be active participants, not passive observers

### Governments Must Build Capacity

- Many governments lack the technical expertise to evaluate AI systems
- "Real hard capacity in government" is needed—not just advisory panels
- Capacity includes: technical talent, evaluation infrastructure, regulatory frameworks
- Investment in government AI expertise is urgent

### Safety and Power Concentration Linked

- As AI becomes more powerful, concentration of capability in few hands increases risk
- Safety failures at scale could have catastrophic consequences
- Governance must address both technical safety and geopolitical dynamics
- Democratizing AI expertise helps distribute risk

### International Cooperation

- AI is a global technology—governance must be international
- UN Security Council as a forum for high-level discussion
- Need for shared standards, information sharing, and coordinated action
- Avoiding a race to the bottom on safety standards
