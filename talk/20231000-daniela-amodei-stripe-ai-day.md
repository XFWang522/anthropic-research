## 完整内容

### 「Anthropic」名称的含义

Daniela Amodei 解释，「anthropic」意为「与人类相关」。公司致力于确保人类始终处于 AI 故事的中心。他们希望人们将 Claude 视为帮助人类完成想做的事、过想过的生活的伙伴或协作者。他们确保人类处于流程中心，无论是基于人类反馈的强化学习，还是更广泛地思考 AI 将如何影响世界。

### 使命与项目聚焦

离开 OpenAI 后，团队必须从零开始构建大型生成式语言模型。第一年重点是让公司运转起来、融资、基础工作，并专注于训练这个大型语言模型。同时，他们在模型之上进行安全研究，力图使其成为市场上最安全的模型。起步时约有六个团队，其中大多数专注于模型之上或直接针对模型的安全工作。他们一直有计划部署产品，但直到大约六到九个月前，主要还是研究机构。

### 物理学家参与的价值

Amodei 的兄弟 Dario（CEO）拥有物理学博士学位，首席科学官 Jared Kaplan 也是联合创始人，同样拥有物理学背景。部分原因是人脉，但她认为团队最知名的工作大多由具有该背景的人完成。团队最著名的两项成果是：开发 GPT-3 的团队，以及撰写关于扩展定律论文的团队——该论文研究如何预测生成式 AI 系统中某些能力何时涌现。这项研究来自将物理学原理应用于 AI。

### 在风暴中保持清醒

Amodei 形容，有时感觉像快速下楼梯——不能想太多，否则会摔倒。但这不意味着缺乏反思。对她来说，重要的是扎根于原则并承担自己的责任。思考整个世界如何反应 AI、不同群体和利益相关者如何与之互动，可能会让人不知所措。因此他们必须 internally 问自己：我们为什么做这件事？为什么创办 Anthropic？答案是：我们希望从第一天起就让 AI 系统更安全。

### 如何向投资者和合作伙伴推销谨慎与深思

Amodei 认为，信任与安全是市场所需要的。他们从道德角度认为这是正确的事，但对企业也有利。当然，有时两者可能冲突，但根据他们的经验，两者可以相当同步。个人和企业不想要产生有害、不诚实或不有用输出的模型。如果有人说「我给你一个语言模型，它会有一半时间对你撒谎，可能产生有毒内容」，没有消费者会说「听起来很棒，我很乐意付钱」。人们希望误报率远低于当前水平，接近零。他们想要最安全的模型版本。

### 日常工作

Amodei 并非天生的早起者，但已调整作息。她几乎每天早上早起锻炼，然后与儿子 Galileo 和丈夫共度时光，这帮助她以好心情开始一天。她尽可能在工作日开始时留出几小时处理大项目和开放思考。其余时间用于会议——她更多是管理者而非创造者日程。包括一对一、团队同步、外部活动、战略与决策会议、招聘等。她总是尽量安排与新员工见面，了解他们在 Anthropic 的体验，但随着公司成长这越来越难。

### 团队规模与 Stripe

开发 Claude v1 时，团队约 60 人。现在接近 140 人，大部分增长来自商业和产品侧，做客户支持、信任与安全。Amodei 表示，在 AI 突然爆发的关注中，Anthropic 选择 Stripe，相信其支付平台的可扩展性和国际聚焦将使其能够服务全球快速增长的用户。

### 核心要点

- **Anthropic = 与人类相关**：名称反映对人类中心 AI 的承诺
- **公益公司**：注册为公益公司，专注于 AI 安全与研究
- **2023 年里程碑**：宣布与谷歌合作、完成 4.5 亿美元融资、发布 Claude（包括 Claude 2）
- **信任与安全作为特性**：应被视为与其他团队平等的合作伙伴，而非进步障碍
- **六团队起步**：多数专注安全研究
- **市场想要安全**：信任与安全是市场所需，道德正确与商业成功可同步

## Full Content

### What's Behind the Name Anthropic

Daniela Amodei explained that one reason for the name is that "anthropic" means relating to humans. A lot of what has been important for them, as they work on evermore powerful generative AI tools that interact with the world, is wanting to make sure humans are still at the center of that story. They hope people use Claude as a partner or a collaborator that helps humans do the things they want to do and live the lives they want to live. They also make sure that humans are at the center of their process, whether it's reinforcement learning from human feedback or just thinking about how AI is going to impact the world more broadly.

### Narrowing the Mission into Projects

When they first left OpenAI, they had to build a large generative language model again from scratch. So their first year was about getting a company up and running, fundraising, the nuts and bolts, focused on training this large language model. But they were also doing the safety research on top of it to try and make it the safest model available in the market. When they started they had something like six teams and the majority of them were working on safety on top of, or directly in, the model. They always had plans to deploy a product, but really until about six to nine months ago, they were a research shop.

### The Benefit of Physicists

Her brother Dario, who's the CEO, has a PhD in physics, and so does their chief science officer, Jared Kaplan, who's also one of their cofounders. So some of it was a network thing. But she also thinks a lot of the work that their team was best known for was done by people with that background. Probably the two things they were best known for is being the team that developed GPT-3 and the team that wrote the paper about scaling laws—about how to predict when certain capabilities will emerge in generative AI systems. That research came from applying physics principles to AI.

### Being a Founder in the Hurricane

She told her husband that sometimes it feels a little bit like when you're running down stairs really quickly. You can't think too hard about it or you're gonna fall. That's not to say they're not self-reflective, because the thing that has felt really important to her is being grounded in their principles and owning their part of it. It can feel overwhelming to try to think about how the whole world is reacting to AI, how different groups and stakeholders are interacting with it. So they have to internally say, why did we do this? Why did we start Anthropic? Which is that they want to help make AI systems safer from day one.

### Selling Investors and Partners on Caution

She thinks trust and safety is something that the market wants. They think this is the correct thing to do from a moral perspective, but it's also good for business. Of course, there might be cases where those two things conflict, but in their experience they've felt like they can be fairly synchronous. Individuals and businesses don't want models that are producing harmful, dishonest, or unhelpful outputs. If you're like, "Hey, can I give you a language model? It will lie to you about half the time, and it will probably produce toxic content," no consumer is going to say, "that sounds great, I would love to pay you for that." People are saying they want the false positive rate to be much closer to zero than what's currently available. They want the safest version of the model.

### What Her Days Look Like

She's not naturally a morning person but she's shifted her schedule to be one. She works out almost every morning, early in the morning, and then spends some time with her son, Galileo, and her husband. It helps her go into the day in a good mood. She tries as much as possible to have a couple of hours free first thing during the work day to tackle big projects and have some open thinking time. The rest of her day is tied up in meetings—she has much more of a manager versus a maker schedule. It's a combo of one on ones, team syncs, external events, strategy and decision-making meetings, recruiting, and other things. She always tries to build in some space to meet with new hires and hear about their experience at Anthropic, though this is getting harder as they grow!

### Team Size and Stripe

When they developed Claude v1, they were about 60 people. They're now closer to 140, and most of that growth has been on the business and product side, doing customer support and trust and safety. Amid this growth, Anthropic has turned to Stripe—trusting that the scalability and international focus of Stripe's payments platform will allow it to serve a rapidly growing number of customers worldwide.

### Key Takeaways

- **Anthropic = Relating to Humans**: The name reflects commitment to human-centric AI
- **Public Benefit Corporation**: Registered as a PBC, specializing in AI safety and research
- **2023 Milestones**: Announced partnership with Google, closed $450M funding round, released Claude (including Claude 2)
- **Trust and Safety as Feature**: Should be viewed as "an equal partner to all of these other teams" rather than an obstacle to progress
- **Six Teams at Start**: Majority focused on safety research
- **Market Wants Safety**: Trust and safety is what the market wants; moral correctness and business success can align
