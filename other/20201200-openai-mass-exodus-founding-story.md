# OpenAI 大规模出走与 Anthropic 创立故事

**时期：** 2020年12月 – 2021年1月  
**主题：** 高级研究人员离开 OpenAI 及 Anthropic 的创立

---

## 概述

2020年12月至2021年1月间，约 **11–12 名**高级研究人员离开 OpenAI，被称为"大规模出走"。该群体随后创立 Anthropic，一家专注于 AI 安全与 alignment 的公益公司。

---

以下个人在此期间离开 OpenAI：

| 姓名 | OpenAI 角色 |
|------|------|
| **Dario Amodei** | 研究副总裁 |

| **Tom Brown** | GPT-3 负责人 |
| **Chris Olah** | 可解释性研究 |
| **Amanda Askell** | 研究 |
| **Jared Kaplan** | 研究 |
| **Jack Clark** | 政策/传播 |
| **Ben Mann** | 研究 |

---

## 离职动机

### 非金钱或 Microsoft

离职**并非**由薪酬担忧或反对 OpenAI 与 Microsoft 合作驱动。研究人员与 OpenAI 方向存在更深层的哲学和战略分歧。

### AI 商业化 vs 理解

出走群体认为 AI 正在**被商业化的速度超过被理解的速度**。他们感到：

- AI 系统在 adequate 安全和可解释性研究之前就被大规模部署
- 商业化速度超过了确保系统 robust 和 steerable 的能力
- 优先顺序偏向产品开发而非 fundamental 安全研究

### 安全优先

核心担忧是 OpenAI **未将安全**相对于能力和部署置于足够优先。研究人员希望建立一个安全、可解释性和 robustness 是使命核心而非次要的组织。

### 愿景分歧

如一位消息人士所言：*"与别人的愿景争论极其 unproductive。"* 与其继续内部辩论，该群体选择建立与自己愿景 alignment 的新组织。

---

## 正式创立

Anthropic 于 **2021年5月** 在旧金山**正式创立为特拉华州公益公司（PBC）**。选择 PBC 结构是为了在法律上将公共利益承诺与利润并置。

# OpenAI Mass Exodus and Anthropic Founding Story

**Period:** December 2020 – January 2021  
**Topic:** The departure of senior researchers from OpenAI and the founding of Anthropic

---

## Overview

Between December 2020 and January 2021, approximately 11–12 senior researchers left OpenAI in what became known as a "mass exodus." This group went on to found Anthropic, a public benefit corporation focused on AI safety and alignment.

---

## Key Departures

The following individuals left OpenAI during this period:

| Name | Role at OpenAI |
|------|----------------|
| **Dario Amodei** | VP of Research |
| **Daniela Amodei** | Operations/Leadership |
| **Tom Brown** | GPT-3 Lead |
| **Chris Olah** | Interpretability Research |
| **Amanda Askell** | Research |
| **Jared Kaplan** | Research |
| **Jack Clark** | Policy/Communications |
| **Ben Mann** | Research |
| **Sam McCandlish** | Research |

---

## Motivations for Leaving

### Not About Money or Microsoft

The departures were **not** driven by compensation concerns or opposition to OpenAI's partnership with Microsoft. The researchers had deeper philosophical and strategic disagreements with OpenAI's direction.

### AI Commercialization vs. Understanding

The departing group believed that AI was being **commercialized faster than it was understood**. They felt that:

- AI systems were being deployed at scale before adequate safety and interpretability research
- The pace of commercialization outpaced the ability to ensure systems were robust and steerable
- Prioritization favored product development over fundamental safety research

### Safety Priorities

A central concern was that OpenAI was **not prioritizing safety** sufficiently relative to capabilities and deployment. The researchers wanted to build an organization where safety, interpretability, and robustness were core to the mission, not secondary.

### Vision Disagreement

As one source put it: *"Incredibly unproductive to argue with someone else's vision."* Rather than continue internal debates, the group chose to build a new organization aligned with their own vision.

---

## Formal Founding

Anthropic was **formally founded as a Delaware Public Benefit Corporation (PBC)** in **May 2021** in San Francisco. The PBC structure was chosen to legally embed a commitment to public benefit alongside profit.
