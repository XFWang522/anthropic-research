# Anthropic D 轮：7.5亿美元

**时间：** 2024年2月  
**轮次：** D 轮  
**金额：** 7.5亿美元  
**估值：** 177亿美元  
**月营收：** 约 800万美元

---

## 概述

Anthropic 于 2024年2月完成 **D 轮** 融资 **7.5亿美元**。该轮由 **Menlo Ventures** 领投，公司估值 **177亿美元**。当时 Anthropic 的月营收约 **800万美元**。

---

## 投资详情

| 指标 | 数值 |
|--------|-------|
| **轮次规模** | 7.5亿美元 |
| **领投** | Menlo Ventures |
| **估值** | 177亿美元 |
| **月营收** | 约 800万美元 |

---

## 领投方

**Menlo Ventures** – 硅谷风险投资公司，投资包括 Uber、Roku、Poshmark 等公司。

---

## 背景

- Claude 3 于 2024年3月发布（Opus、Sonnet、Haiku）
- 企业采用正在增长
- 与 OpenAI、Google 等的竞争加剧
- Anthropic 正在扩展 API 和产品线

---

## 营收背景

在约 800万美元月营收下，Anthropic 的年 run rate 约 **9600万美元**。177亿美元估值意味着 significant 营收倍数，反映投资者对快速增长的预期。

---

## 资金用途

D 轮资金支持：

- 模型开发和训练的扩展
- 企业销售和客户成功
- API 基础设施和全球扩张
- 安全研究和政策

# Anthropic Series D: $750 Million

**Date:** February 2024  
**Round:** Series D  
**Amount:** $750 million  
**Valuation:** $17.7 billion  
**Monthly revenue:** ~$8 million

---

## Overview

Anthropic closed its **Series D** funding round of **$750 million** in February 2024. The round was **led by Menlo Ventures**, valuing the company at **$17.7 billion**. At the time, Anthropic's monthly revenue was approximately **$8 million**.

---

## Investment Details

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Round size** | $750 million |
| **Lead investor** | Menlo Ventures |
| **Valuation** | $17.7 billion |
| **Monthly revenue** | ~$8 million |

---

## Lead Investor

**Menlo Ventures** – A Silicon Valley venture capital firm with investments in companies such as Uber, Roku, and Poshmark.

---

## Context

- Claude 3 had been released (Opus, Sonnet, Haiku) in March 2024
- Enterprise adoption was growing
- Competition with OpenAI, Google, and others was intensifying
- Anthropic was scaling API and product offerings

---

## Revenue Context

At ~$8 million monthly revenue, Anthropic's annual run rate was approximately **$96 million**. The $17.7 billion valuation implied a significant revenue multiple, reflecting investor expectations for rapid growth.

---

## Use of Funds

The Series D capital supported:

- Scaling of model development and training
- Enterprise sales and customer success
- API infrastructure and global expansion
- Safety research and policy
