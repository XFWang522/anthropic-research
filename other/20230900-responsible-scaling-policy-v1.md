# 负责任扩展政策（RSP）v1.0

**时间：** 2023年9月  
**主题：** Anthropic 的初始负责任扩展政策框架

---

## 概述

2023年9月，Anthropic 发布**负责任扩展政策（RSP）v1.0**，这是一个管理与**灾难性风险**相关的 AI 发展的框架。该政策定义了从 **ASL-1** 到 **ASL-3+** 的 **ASL 等级**（AI 安全等级），并确立了扩展条件。

---

## AI 安全等级（ASL）

RSP 引入了分层 AI 安全等级体系：

| 等级 | 描述 |
|-------|-------------|
| **ASL-1** | 低风险系统；基线保障 |
| **ASL-2** | 中等风险；增强安全措施 |
| **ASL-3** | 高风险；重大限制和评估 |
| **ASL-3+** | 灾难性风险阈值；严格限制 |

---

## 灾难性风险框架

RSP 围绕**灾难性风险**——即 AI 系统可能造成严重、广泛伤害的场景——构建扩展决策。关键要素包括：

- **风险评估** – 评估模型的危险能力
- **扩展条件** – 在安全措施到位前暂停或限制扩展
- **透明度** – 安全评估和决策的公开报告

---

## 核心承诺

- **不安全则暂停** – 当模型接近灾难性风险阈值时承诺暂停或限制扩展
- **安全优先于能力** – 将安全置于竞争压力之上
- **评估与监控** – 对模型能力和风险的持续评估

---

## 背景

- RSP 是 Anthropic 的差异化因素；鲜有其他实验室发布可比政策
- 该政策在 AI 安全和政策辩论中具有影响力
- 后续版本（如 2026年2月的 RSP v3.0）因 perceived 削弱承诺而招致批评

# Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP) v1.0

**Date:** September 2023  
**Topic:** Anthropic's initial Responsible Scaling Policy framework

---

## Overview

In **September 2023**, Anthropic released **Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP) v1.0**, a framework for managing AI development in relation to **catastrophic risks**. The policy defined **ASL levels** (AI Safety Levels) from **ASL-1** through **ASL-3+** and established conditions for scaling.

---

## AI Safety Levels (ASL)

The RSP introduced a tiered system of AI Safety Levels:

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **ASL-1** | Lower-risk systems; baseline safeguards |
| **ASL-2** | Moderate risk; enhanced safety measures |
| **ASL-3** | High risk; significant restrictions and evaluations |
| **ASL-3+** | Catastrophic risk threshold; strict limits |

---

## Catastrophic Risks Framework

The RSP framed scaling decisions around **catastrophic risks**—scenarios where AI systems could cause severe, widespread harm. Key elements included:

- **Risk assessments** – Evaluating models for dangerous capabilities
- **Scaling conditions** – Pausing or limiting scaling until safety measures are in place
- **Transparency** – Public reporting on safety evaluations and decisions

---

## Core Commitments

- **Pause if unsafe** – Commitment to pause or limit scaling when models approach catastrophic risk thresholds
- **Safety before capabilities** – Prioritizing safety over competitive pressure
- **Evaluation and monitoring** – Ongoing assessment of model capabilities and risks

---

## Context

- RSP was a differentiator for Anthropic; few other labs had published comparable policies
- The policy was influential in AI safety and policy debates
- Later versions (e.g., RSP v3.0 in Feb 2026) drew criticism for perceived weakening of commitments
