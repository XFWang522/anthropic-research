# 作者版权诉讼（Bartz 诉 Anthropic）

**时间：** 2024年提起；2025年9月和解  
**案件：** Bartz v. Anthropic PBC  
**主题：** 涉嫌使用盗版书籍训练；15亿美元和解

---

## 概述

**Bartz v. Anthropic PBC** 是一起集体诉讼，指控 Anthropic 使用来自 **LibGen**（Library Genesis）影子图书馆的**盗版书籍**训练其模型。诉讼称 Anthropic 在未经适当授权的情况下使用了**多达 700 万本书**。联邦法官裁定**合理使用**适用于合法获取的作品，但**不适用于盗版材料**。该案**被认证为集体诉讼**，Anthropic 于 **2025年9月** 达成 **15亿美元（$1.5 billion）和解**。

---

## 指控

| 指控 | 详情 |
|------------|--------|
| **训练数据来源** | 来自 LibGen 的盗版书籍 |
| **规模** | 多达 700 万本书 |
| **法律理论** | 版权侵权；合理使用不适用于盗版作品 |

---

## 关键裁决

法官区分了：

- **合法获取的作品** – 合理使用可能适用于训练 AI 模型
- **盗版作品** – 合理使用不适用；使用盗版材料构成侵权

该裁决对更广泛的 AI 训练数据辩论具有影响。

---

## 集体诉讼认证

法院**将该案认证为集体诉讼**，允许大量作者集体追索索赔。这增加了潜在责任和解压力。

---

## 和解

| 详情 | 金额 |
|--------|-------|
| **和解金额** | 15亿美元（$1.5 billion） |
| **日期** | 2025年9月 |
| **状态** | 已解决 |

---

## 影响

- **训练数据来源** – AI 公司面临对训练数据来源的更多审查
- **影子图书馆** – 使用 LibGen、Sci-Hub 及类似来源存在法律风险
- **行业先例** – 其他 AI 公司面临类似诉讼；和解和裁决可能塑造行业实践
- **授权** – 使用授权或合成数据进行训练的压力

# Authors Copyright Lawsuit (Bartz v. Anthropic)

**Date:** Filed 2024; settled September 2025  
**Case:** Bartz v. Anthropic PBC  
**Topic:** Alleged use of pirated books for training; $1.5 billion settlement

---

## Overview

**Bartz v. Anthropic PBC** was a class action lawsuit alleging that Anthropic trained its models on **pirated books** from **LibGen** (Library Genesis), a shadow library. The suit claimed Anthropic used **up to 7 million books** without proper licensing. A federal judge ruled that **fair use** applied to legally obtained works but **not to pirated materials**. The case was **certified as a class action**, and Anthropic reached a **$1.5 billion settlement** in **September 2025**.

---

## Allegations

| Allegation | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| **Source of training data** | Pirated books from LibGen |
| **Scale** | Up to 7 million books |
| **Legal theory** | Copyright infringement; fair use does not apply to pirated works |

---

## Key Ruling

The judge distinguished between:

- **Legally obtained works** – Fair use may apply for training AI models
- **Pirated works** – Fair use does not apply; use of pirated materials constitutes infringement

This ruling had implications for the broader AI training data debate.

---

## Class Action Certification

The court **certified the case as a class action**, allowing a large group of authors to pursue claims collectively. This increased the potential liability and settlement pressure.

---

## Settlement

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Settlement amount** | $1.5 billion |
| **Date** | September 2025 |
| **Status** | Resolved |

---

## Implications

- **Training data provenance** – AI companies face increased scrutiny of training data sources
- **Shadow libraries** – Use of LibGen, Sci-Hub, and similar sources carries legal risk
- **Industry precedent** – Other AI companies face similar lawsuits; settlements and rulings may shape industry practice
- **Licensing** – Pressure to use licensed or synthetic data for training
